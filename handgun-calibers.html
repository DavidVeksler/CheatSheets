Modern Handgun Calibers Cheatsheet

A comprehensive, interactive guide to popular handgun ammunition. Compare calibers by their ballistic characteristics, recoil profile, capacity, and common applications for self-defense, sport shooting, and concealed carry.

Last Updated: 2024-03-27

Rimfire & Subcompact Calibers

.22 Long Rifle (.22 LR)

Known for its minimal recoil and low cost, the .22 LR is an ideal cartridge for training, recreational plinking, and introducing new shooters to firearms.

While not a primary choice for self-defense due to limited stopping power, advancements in bullet technology have created more effective loads for specific applications.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.223 inches
Typical Bullet Weight30-40 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity1,080-1,640 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy100-200 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Beginner Training
  • Small Game Hunting
  • Recreational "Plinking"
.380 ACP

A popular choice for small, lightweight concealed carry pistols due to its compact size and manageable recoil.

While traditionally viewed as having less stopping power than larger calibers, modern self-defense ammunition (e.g., jacketed hollow points) has significantly improved its terminal effectiveness.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.355 inches
Typical Bullet Weight85-100 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity900-1,050 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy170-220 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Concealed Carry (CCW)
  • Backup Handguns
  • Recoil-Sensitive Shooters

Duty & Defense Calibers

9mm Luger

The world's most popular handgun caliber, offering a superb balance of magazine capacity, manageable recoil, and effective terminal performance.

Widely adopted by military, law enforcement, and civilians, the 9mm is a versatile round with a vast selection of affordable ammunition. Modern hollow-point designs have made it an excellent self-defense choice.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.355 inches
Typical Bullet Weight115-147 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity900-1,500 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy335-400 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Self-Defense / Home Defense
  • Law Enforcement / Military
  • Competition Shooting
.40 S&W

Designed as a compromise between 9mm and .45 ACP, offering more energy than 9mm but with a sharper, more pronounced felt recoil.

Formerly popular with law enforcement (e.g., the FBI), it has seen a decline as 9mm bullet technology has improved. It remains a potent and viable self-defense caliber.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.400 inches
Typical Bullet Weight155-180 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity850-1,330 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy350-500 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Self-Defense
  • Law Enforcement (Legacy)
  • Competition (USPSA Limited)
.45 ACP

A classic American cartridge famed for its stopping power. It fires a heavy, large-diameter bullet at subsonic speeds, creating a strong but 'pushy' recoil.

With a legendary military service record in the M1911 pistol, the .45 ACP is a proven performer. The trade-off for its power is typically a lower magazine capacity compared to 9mm handguns of similar size.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.452 inches
Typical Bullet Weight185-230 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity800-1,200 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy350-450 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Self-Defense / Home Defense
  • Military (Historical)
  • Suppressor Host (naturally subsonic)

High-Power & Specialty Calibers

10mm Auto

The pinnacle of power in a common semi-automatic platform, delivering near-magnum performance with a flat trajectory and high muzzle energy.

The power of the 10mm Auto comes with significant recoil, making it challenging for some shooters. It's a top choice for handgun hunting and defense against large predators in the backcountry.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.400 inches
Typical Bullet Weight135-220 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity1,100-1,600 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy500-750+ ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Handgun Hunting
  • Backcountry / Predator Defense
  • Enthusiast Target Shooting
5.7x28mm

A unique, high-velocity bottlenecked cartridge known for its flat trajectory, low recoil, and high penetration capabilities with specific loads.

Designed by FN Herstal for Personal Defense Weapons (PDWs), it looks like a miniature rifle cartridge. Its terminal performance is a topic of debate, but it excels at high capacity and ease of shooting.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.224 inches
Typical Bullet Weight27-40 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity1,700-2,350 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy195-394 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Personal Defense Weapon (PDW)
  • Target Shooting
  • Varmint Hunting

Classic Revolver Calibers

.38 Special

A venerable revolver cartridge prized for its inherent accuracy and mild recoil. It remains a popular choice for small-frame revolvers and new shooters.

While standard loads are modest, higher pressure +P (Plus P) ammunition offers significantly improved performance for self-defense applications.

Ballistic Metrics
Bullet Diameter.357 inches
Typical Bullet Weight110-158 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity755-950 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy200-300 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Concealed Carry (Revolvers)
  • Beginner Training
  • Target Shooting
.357 Magnum

A powerful and versatile revolver cartridge developed from the .38 Special, offering a major leap in velocity and terminal ballistics.

The .357 Magnum is a proven man-stopper and an effective hunting round for medium game. Its recoil is substantial, but revolvers chambered for it have the advantage of also being able to fire the milder .38 Special ammunition.

Ballistic Metrics (from Pistol)
Bullet Diameter.357 inches
Typical Bullet Weight125-180 grains
Typical Muzzle Velocity1,200-1,500 fps
Typical Muzzle Energy400-700 ft-lbs
Common Uses
  • Self-Defense / Home Defense
  • Handgun Hunting (Medium Game)
  • Law Enforcement (Historical)
