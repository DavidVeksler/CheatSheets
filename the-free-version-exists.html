The Pattern

Before paying for anything government-adjacent, financial-adjacent, health-adjacent, or paperwork-adjacent, run the reflex search: the thing you want + site:.gov. The paid intermediary often buys the ad slot above the official page, copies the official form, and charges you for typing your data into the free path.

The strongest cases are preventive tools that beat paid monitoring. A credit freeze blocks most new-account fraud for $0; credit monitoring usually tells you after someone tried. Other cases are simply public goods: libraries, court self-help centers, consumer complaint portals, public-domain books, and official forms.

Common Mistakes

Paying the sponsored result

Search the thing plus site:.gov or start from USAGov before entering payment details.

Assuming free means scam

For public goods, the inversion is often true: the official free version is safer than the paid lookalike.

Taking the “free trial” bait

A trial that converts to paid is not the same as an official no-cost path.

Letting a finder take your money

Unclaimed-property finders may charge a cut of money a state database lets you claim directly.

Forgetting benefits you already pay for

Credit-card warranties, rental coverage, trip delay terms, and library access are often prepaid through annual fees or taxes.

WORTH IT

When Paid Genuinely Wins

Complex tax returns

Real CPAs and enrolled agents earn their fee when you have business income, rentals, multi-state issues, stock compensation, or notices.

Contested legal matters

Free forms are not litigation strategy. Attorneys matter when rights, custody, housing, immigration, or large assets are disputed.

Real deadlines

A legitimate passport expediter or courier can be rational if the deadline is real and the agency path cannot meet it.

Family and team security

Paid password-manager plans are often worth it for shared vaults, recovery, emergency access, admin controls, and support.
