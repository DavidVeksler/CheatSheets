Bitcoin Fortress: HNW Self-Custody Protocol

Inspired by Jameson Lopp's Principles for Real-World Threat Mitigation

A comprehensive security framework for high net worth Bitcoin holders focused on layered, geographically-diverse controls to deter attackers and ensure recovery.

Last Updated: January 2025 | Target: Significant Bitcoin Holdings

Guiding Philosophy (Lopp-Inspired)

Security is about layered, geographically-diverse controls making you an unattractive, time-consuming target. Controls must be robust AND simple enough to execute under duress. Avoid over-engineering; prioritize recoverability by you or your heirs.

Critical Reality Check

Your Bitcoin is worthless if you're dead, kidnapped, or lose access. This guide prioritizes survival, access, and recoverability over theoretical perfection.

Threat Tiering for HNW Individuals

Consider threats from opportunistic to targeted, including physical coercion and state-level actors for large holdings.

Critical: Self-inflicted loss / Bad Backups
High: Targeted Physical Coercion / Robbery
Medium: Sophisticated Remote Attacks / Sim Swaps
Low: Opportunistic Malware / Phishing

Operational Security (OPSEC) & Privacy

Silence is Armor CRITICAL

Never discuss balances, signing locations, or travel plans publicly. Most violent attacks start with doxxing or bragging.

Zero public clues: No talk of holdings, security posture, or crypto wealth.
Implementation:
  • Segregate identities: Use pseudonyms, burner phones/emails for KYC if possible, separate addresses for node traffic.
  • Use P.O. Boxes or business addresses for hardware deliveries.
  • Communicate sensitive information ONLY via E2E encrypted messengers (e.g., Signal).
  • Tell persistent questioners you "sold," "lost it," or "only dabble."

Ref: Lopp on OpSec, Casa Blog

Address & Lifestyle Privacy HIGH

Minimize links between your identity, physical address, and Bitcoin. Reduce your attack surface.

Protection Layers:
  • LLC + registered agent for property.
  • Secure mail forwarding for crypto mail.
  • Remove PII from data brokers (DeleteMe, Optery).
  • Vary hardware wallet delivery addresses.
  • Avoid overt displays of wealth.

Physical Security & "$5 Wrench" Mitigation

Time-Delay Deterrence HIGH

Core principle: If no single site holds enough keys for quorum, an attacker cannot get what they want quickly. This is your primary defense against coercion.

Lopp's Insight: The goal is to make extraction so slow and complex that attackers move to softer targets.
Implementation:
  • Geographically distribute multisig keys/seed shares (see Technical Security).
  • Use bank SDBs, trusted custodians in different jurisdictions.
  • Consider time-locks on a portion of funds if appropriate (advanced).
  • Have a duress wallet/PIN that reveals a small, believable amount.

Ref: Lopp on Time-Delay, Casa Blog

Home Fortress Protocol HIGH

Harden your perimeter like a small bank. Make breaking in noisy, slow, and highly visible.

Minimum Setup:
  • Reinforced doors, shatter-proof window film, robust locks.
  • Monitored alarm system with cellular backup.
  • Visible and hidden cameras (PoE, NVR + cloud).
  • Safe room or reinforced master bedroom.
Study Real Attacks: Lopp curates an archive of 200+ physical attacks. Learn from them. (Lopp's GitHub)

Ref: Casa Guide to Physical Security, WSJ on crypto attacks

Technical Security: Custody Architecture

Multi-Signature (Multisig) Dominance CRITICAL

For HNW: 3-of-5 or 5-of-7 multisig is default. No single person (including you) can unilaterally spend. Keys must be geographically diverse.

Lopp's Preference: Multisig over Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS) for better operational usability and auditability.
Key Principles:
  • Heterogeneous Hardware: Use different brands of hardware wallets (Coldcard, Trezor, Seedsigner, etc.) for different keys to avoid single-vendor risk.
  • Geographic Distribution: Keys/seeds stored in different cities, states, or even countries with varying legal/political risk profiles.
  • DIY or Managed: Consider services like Casa (co-founded by Lopp) for a managed solution or model your DIY setup on their Wealth Security Protocol (emergency lockdown, health checks).
  • Test recovery processes rigorously and regularly.

Ref: Lopp's Cypherpunk Cogitations, Casa Wealth Security Protocol

Seed Backup Resilience: Metal & Depth CRITICAL

Use stainless 316L or titanium plates (min 5mm thick, one-piece). Paper/thin aluminum fail fire/flood/crush tests.

Backup Strategy:
  • Multiple Copies, Diverse Hazards: At least two offline, tamper-evident metal backups in different hazard zones (e.g., one fire-proof safe at home, another buried or in a bank SDB far away).
  • Shamir Shares on Metal: If using SSS (despite Lopp's preference for multisig usability), each share must be on robust metal.
  • Burial Cache (Extreme): For ultimate resilience, Lopp demonstrated PVC-encapsulated burial.
    • Use clear landmark-based navigation for retrieval.
    • Bury >30cm deep to evade casual metal detectors.
    • Leave written retrieval instructions for heirs.
Test Your Backups: Annually verify accessibility and integrity of all backup copies.

Ref: Lopp's Treatise on Seed Backup Design, How to Bury a Seed

Inheritance & Succession: Plan To Die

Heirs Protocol: Clarity & Practice CRITICAL

Your Bitcoin is lost if heirs can't recover it. Heirs' unfamiliarity with hardware wallets is the #1 failure mode. Document, rehearse, simplify.

Multi-Layered Inheritance (Lopp-Inspired):
  1. Legal Layer: Trust documents naming successor trustees, specific digital asset instructions. Consult crypto-aware estate attorney.
  2. Technical Access for Heirs:
    • Detailed, plain-language signing procedures.
    • Rehearsal video demonstrating recovery (use a test wallet).
    • Distribute instructions/video to trusted executors/attorney in sealed, tamper-evident envelopes or encrypted files.
    • Consider models like Casa's Inheritance vault: heir holds one key, trusted third-party (like Casa or attorney) holds another, potential time-delay for release.
  3. Practical Training & Support:
    • Annual "fire drill" with primary heir(s)/trustee.
    • Embed an expert recovery service or trusted, Bitcoin-literate advisor in the plan.
    • Clear "First Call" instructions for heirs.
Lopp's Warning: "I'll teach them someday" means heirs likely get nothing. Action today is paramount.

Ref: Casa Inheritance Product, Lopp on Heir Unfamiliarity

Continuous Verification & Drills

Routine Practice & Adaptation MEDIUM

Security is not static. Schedule quarterly sign-and-verify drills. Health-check devices, rotate batteries, confirm backup accessibility. Re-evaluate your threat model after major life events.

Key Maintenance Activities:
  • Quarterly Drills:
    • Perform a small transaction requiring your multisig quorum.
    • Verify each hardware wallet powers on and is functional.
    • Check firmware versions; update on an airgapped machine if necessary, after verifying update authenticity.
    • Inspect physical condition of metal backups.
  • Annual Full Review:
    • Attempt a full seed recovery on a test device for at least one key.
    • Review all documentation for clarity and accuracy.
    • Re-assess counterparty risk if using custodians for any keys.
  • Life Event Triggers:
    • Marriage, divorce, birth of children.
    • Moving to a new home, city, or country.
    • Significant changes in tax law or regulations in your jurisdictions.
    • New known attack vectors or vulnerabilities discussed in the Bitcoin community.
Lopp's Principle: Routine drills keep entropy (and heirs' confusion) on your side. Automation (like Casa's health checks) is good; calendared DIY checks are essential.
