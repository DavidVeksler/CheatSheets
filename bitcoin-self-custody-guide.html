Operational Security (OPSEC)

The First Rule of Bitcoin Club CRITICAL

Nobody should know you own Bitcoin. Period. Most attacks start with information leakage.

Never reveal holdings: Not even "I own some crypto" at parties. This is non-negotiable for HNW individuals.
Implementation:
  • Create separate "crypto" vs "normie" online personas (social media, email).
  • Use strong pseudonyms for all crypto-related activities.
  • Never post wallet screenshots, transaction IDs, or balance information.
  • Decline all podcast/media appearances about your "crypto journey" or wealth.
  • If pressed by casual acquaintances or family, feign disinterest or imply you "lost it all in a crash" or "sold long ago."
Real Attack Vector:

Jameson Lopp documented 100+ physical Bitcoin attacks. Over 90% of identifiable cases began with public knowledge of the victim's Bitcoin holdings.

Address & Lifestyle Privacy HIGH

Linking your home address or lavish lifestyle to Bitcoin ownership creates a targeted physical attack risk.

Protection Layers:
  • Use an LLC and registered agent service for property ownership to obscure personal details.
  • Utilize a P.O. Box or secure mail forwarding service (e.g., Traveling Mailbox) for all crypto-related mail, including hardware wallet purchases.
  • Never use your home address for exchange KYC; use an office address or P.O. Box if permissible and secure.
  • Actively remove your personal information from public records and data broker sites (use services like DeleteMe, Optery, or Kanary).
  • Use different billing and shipping addresses for hardware wallet purchases; consider shipping to a secure office or mail service.
  • Avoid ostentatious displays of wealth that could draw unwanted attention.
Pro Tip:

Consider establishing residency or LLCs in privacy-friendly jurisdictions (e.g., Wyoming, Delaware in the US) for enhanced asset protection and privacy, if legally appropriate for your situation.

Physical Security

Home Fortress Protocol HIGH

Layered physical security deters and delays intruders, especially against $5 wrench attacks.

Minimum Setup ($5K-15K investment):
  • Perimeter: High fence (8ft+), secure gates, motion-activated floodlights, clear "Private Property" and "Beware of Dog" (even if figurative) signage.
  • Cameras: High-resolution (4K) PoE camera system with local NVR (30+ day recording) and encrypted cloud backup. Visible and hidden cameras.
  • Doors & Windows: Grade 1 deadbolts, reinforced door frames and strike plates, security film on ground-floor windows and glass doors.
  • Alarm System: Professionally monitored alarm with cellular backup, multiple sensor types (door/window, motion, glass break).
  • Safe Room (Recommended): Reinforced master bedroom or dedicated room with solid core door, upgraded locks, and potentially a separate alarm zone. Communications (e.g., burner phone) inside.
Advanced Setup ($15K+ investment):
  • Ballistic panels or reinforced walls in critical areas.
  • Panic buttons (wireless/hardwired) in key rooms, linked to alarm system.
  • License Plate Recognition (LPR) cameras at property entry points.
  • Professional security consultant assessment for tailored recommendations.
Reality Check: The goal is to create a 5-15 minute delay for police response and make your home a harder target, not to build an impenetrable Fort Knox. Deterrence is key.
Personal Defense & Duress Protocol MEDIUM

When physical security layers are breached, your primary goal is survival. Compliance combined with misdirection can save lives.

Under Duress Protocol (Prioritize Life):
  1. Comply Immediately: Your life is infinitely more valuable than your Bitcoin. Do not resist.
  2. Decoy Wallet: Have a readily accessible hardware wallet (or software wallet on a phone) with a plausible but not insignificant amount of crypto (e.g., $5K-$20K). This wallet is meant to be surrendered.
  3. Duress Passphrase/PIN: If your hardware wallet supports it (e.g., Coldcard, some Trezor models with plausible deniability features), use a duress passphrase that unlocks the decoy wallet.
  4. Time-Locked Explanations: "My main funds are in a time-locked smart contract / multisig setup that requires X hours/days to access." (This should be true if possible, or a practiced, believable lie).
  5. Geographic Distribution Story: "Other keys/seeds are stored in a bank safety deposit box / with a lawyer in another city, I can't access them immediately."
Preparation:
  • Situational awareness training (e.g., Krav Maga, tactical defense courses focus heavily on this). Physical fighting is a last resort.
  • If legal and you are trained: concealed carry permit and regular practice.
  • Emergency medical training (e.g., "Stop the Bleed" course).
  • Mentally rehearse duress scenarios. Practice your responses. Family members should also be aware of basic duress protocols (e.g., comply, don't escalate).

Technical Security Architecture

Multi-Signature (Multisig) Setup CRITICAL

Single points of failure (one seed, one device) are single points of catastrophic loss. Multisig mitigates this.

Recommended: 2-of-3 or 3-of-5 Multisig

This means multiple keys are needed to authorize a transaction, but not all keys are required (allowing for loss/compromise of one or more keys depending on setup).

Example 2-of-3 Setup:
  • Key 1: Hardware wallet (e.g., Coldcard) stored securely at home for relatively quick access.
  • Key 2: Hardware wallet (e.g., Trezor) stored in a bank safety deposit box or with a trusted custodian in a different location.
  • Key 3: Hardware wallet (e.g., Ledger) or offline steel backup of seed, stored with a specialized attorney or trust company, potentially in another jurisdiction.
Setup Requirements & Best Practices:
  • Diverse Hardware: Use hardware wallets from different manufacturers to protect against supply chain attacks or manufacturer-specific vulnerabilities.
  • Air-Gapped Generation: Generate keys on fully air-gapped devices whenever possible.
  • Test Thoroughly: Perform small test transactions, and crucially, test the full seed phrase recovery process for each key and the multisig wallet backup itself (e.g., Sparrow Wallet file, Caravan config). Do this quarterly.
  • Document for Inheritors: Clearly document the setup, locations of keys/seeds, and recovery procedures for your heirs. This documentation itself needs secure storage.

Seed Phrase Backup Strategy CRITICAL

Paper burns, ink fades, memory fails. Your seed phrases require robust, durable backups.

Distribution & Splitting Strategy:
  • Never store a complete seed phrase in one single location.
  • Shamir Secret Sharing (SSS): Split a seed into multiple shares (e.g., 3-of-5, where any 3 shares can reconstruct the seed). Use a reputable tool for this (e.g., Seedtool, some hardware wallets).
    • Store shares on steel, geographically distributed.
  • Geographic Distribution: Store individual (if multisig) or SSS shares in physically separate, secure locations (e.g., home safe, bank SDB, trusted friend/family in another city/country, lawyer).
  • One copy of instructions/key locations with a trusted attorney or in a Dead Man's Switch system.
Test Your Backups Annually: Attempt to restore a small wallet using your backups. A significant portion of "lost" Bitcoin is due to failed, untested, or incorrectly created backups.

Inheritance & Succession Planning

Dead Man's Switch & Heirs Protocol CRITICAL

Your Bitcoin can easily be lost forever upon your death or incapacitation unless you establish a robust technical, legal, and practical inheritance plan.

Multi-Layered Inheritance System:
  1. Legal Layer:
    • Establish a revocable or irrevocable trust. The trust can "own" an LLC which in turn holds the Bitcoin. This can offer privacy and probate avoidance.
    • Clearly name successor trustees who are capable and trustworthy.
    • Include specific instructions for digital asset handling in your will and trust documents. Consult a crypto-aware estate attorney.
  2. Technical Layer (Access for Heirs):
    • Securely store essential information with your attorney or a trusted corporate trustee: types of wallets used, derivation paths (if non-standard), basic recovery steps, locations of seed phrases/keys, and contact information for a crypto expert if needed. This could be in a sealed envelope, or via a service.
    • Consider using services like Unchained Capital or Casa that offer specific inheritance protocols for multisig setups.
    • For a portion of funds, a time-locked transaction (nLockTime) could release Bitcoin to heirs after a certain period, but this is complex and has its own risks.
  3. Practical Layer (Guidance for Heirs):
    • Conduct an annual "Bitcoin fire drill" with your primary heir(s) or trustee. Explain the process without revealing actual seeds unless absolutely necessary for their role. Show them where instructions are.
    • Create a detailed, step-by-step video walkthrough of the recovery process (for a test wallet). Store this securely with your attorney or in an encrypted, distributed manner.
    • Appoint a Bitcoin-literate executor or advisor who can assist your heirs.
    • Provide clear, simple initial instructions for your heirs: "In case of my death/incapacitation, immediately contact [Trusted Attorney Name] at [Number] and [Crypto Expert/Advisor Name] at [Number]. Do NOT try to access anything alone initially."
Procrastination is Theft from Your Heirs: Simply thinking "I'll teach them someday" is a recipe for your heirs receiving nothing or facing immense difficulty and risk.

Disaster Preparedness

Geographic Redundancy MEDIUM

Natural disasters, fires, or regional instability are typically localized. Your Bitcoin recovery materials must not be solely localized.

Distribution Strategy for Backups (Seeds/Keys/Config):
  • Minimum Distance: Aim for at least 100 miles (160 km) between backup locations, ideally in different states or even countries.
  • Diverse Disaster Profiles: Avoid placing all backups in areas prone to the same type of disaster (e.g., all in earthquake zones or flood plains).
  • International Options: Consider a professional custody service or safety deposit box in a stable, privacy-respecting international jurisdiction (e.g., Switzerland, Singapore, Cayman Islands) for one multisig key or SSS share.
  • Cloud (Highly Cautious): Heavily encrypted fragments of backups (e.g., SSS shares) could be stored on reputable cloud services as a last resort, but this carries significant risks if encryption is weak or access is lost. Not recommended for complete seeds.
Access During Crisis / Evacuation:
  • Memorize the access method/location for at least ONE key/seed share that you could retrieve in an emergency.
  • Maintain an emergency fund (cash, precious metals, stablecoins on a separate device) in multiple easily accessible locations or jurisdictions.
  • Have a satellite phone or other off-grid communication device if you are in a remote or disaster-prone area.
  • A "bug-out bag" could contain a hardware wallet with a modest amount of funds, or a steel backup of a specific emergency seed, but this bag itself becomes a target.
Brain Wallet (Extreme Caution) LOW

As an absolute last resort, your mind can serve as ultimate cold storage, but this is extremely risky and suitable only for small, emergency amounts.

Implementation (If You Must):
  • Standard 12-word Seed: Memorize a standard BIP39 12-word seed phrase. Do NOT attempt 24 words (too high error rate). Do NOT create your own words (low entropy).
  • Memory Palace Technique: Use strong mnemonic techniques like the memory palace to associate words with vivid imagery in a familiar location.
  • Small Amount Only: Only for a very small, "disposable" emergency fund (e.g., $1K-$10K). This is NOT for your main holdings.
  • Frequent, Discrete Recitation: Practice recalling the seed phrase weekly or daily, but do so privately and without writing it down during practice.
  • Never Primary: This should *never* be your primary or sole storage method for significant amounts. It's a backup to other backups.
Warning: Head trauma, age-related memory decline, extreme stress, or even a simple lapse in memory can lead to permanent loss. Human memory is fallible.
