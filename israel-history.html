History of Israel

From Antiquity to Today: A Chronological Reference Guide

Ancient Period (c. 1200 BCE – 63 BCE)

c. 1200 - 1000 BCE Biblical Period & Settlement
  • The Exodus & Conquest: According to biblical tradition, Israelites escape Egypt, wander the desert, and conquer Canaan under Joshua.
  • Period of the Judges: A decentralized tribal confederation lacking a central government, led by charismatic leaders known as Judges.
  • Archaeological Context: The Merneptah Stele (c. 1208 BCE) provides the earliest extra-biblical reference to a people called "Israel" in Canaan.
c. 1000 - 930 BCE United Monarchy
  • King Saul: First king of Israel, unifies the tribes against Philistine threats.
  • King David: Captures Jerusalem and makes it the capital. Establishes a vast regional power.
  • King Solomon: Builds the First Temple in Jerusalem. Era characterized by wealth, trade, and extensive building projects.
930 - 586 BCE Divided Kingdom & First Exile
  • The Split: Following Solomon's death, the kingdom divides into the Northern Kingdom (Israel) and Southern Kingdom (Judah).
  • 722 BCE (Assyrian Conquest): The Assyrian Empire destroys the Northern Kingdom, leading to the "Ten Lost Tribes" of Israel.
  • 586 BCE (Babylonian Exile): King Nebuchadnezzar II of Babylon conquers Judah, destroys the First Temple, and exiles the Jewish elite to Babylon.
538 - 63 BCE Persian & Hellenistic Periods
  • 538 BCE: Cyrus the Great of Persia decrees that Jews may return to Zion. The Second Temple is constructed (completed c. 516 BCE).
  • 332 BCE: Alexander the Great conquers the region, initiating the Hellenistic period.
  • 167-160 BCE (Maccabean Revolt): Jewish rebellion against the Seleucid Empire (Antiochus IV), leading to independent Hasmonean dynasty. (Origin of Hanukkah).

Roman & Middle Ages (63 BCE – 1517 CE)

63 BCE - 70 CE Roman Rule & Destruction of Jerusalem
  • 63 BCE: Roman general Pompey captures Jerusalem; Judea becomes a client kingdom of Rome.
  • 37 - 4 BCE: King Herod the Great rules, expanding the Second Temple.
  • 66 - 73 CE (First Jewish-Roman War): The Great Revolt against Rome.
  • 70 CE: Romans (under Titus) breach Jerusalem and destroy the Second Temple.
  • 73 CE: The fall of Masada, marking the end of the revolt.
132 - 636 CE Bar Kokhba, Byzantine & Talmudic Era
  • 132-136 CE (Bar Kokhba Revolt): Second major revolt against Rome. Brutally crushed by Emperor Hadrian, who renames Judea to Syria Palaestina and exiles many Jews.
  • c. 200 - 500 CE: Center of Jewish life shifts to the Galilee. The Mishnah and Jerusalem Talmud are compiled.
  • 324 - 638 CE: Byzantine (Christian) rule. Churches are built over Christian holy sites.
636 - 1517 CE Islamic, Crusader & Mamluk Periods
  • 636 CE: Arab Muslim armies conquer the region. The Dome of the Rock and Al-Aqsa Mosque are built on the Temple Mount.
  • 1099 - 1291 (Crusader Period): European Christians conquer Jerusalem, massacring Jewish and Muslim populations. Establish the Kingdom of Jerusalem.
  • 1291 - 1517 (Mamluk Period): Egyptian Mamluks expel the Crusaders and rule the region, which falls into economic and demographic decline.

Ottoman Rule & British Mandate (1517 – 1948)

1517 - 1917 The Ottoman Empire
  • Early Ottoman Rule: Suleiman the Magnificent rebuilds the walls of Jerusalem (which stand today).
  • Late 19th Century: The region is largely rural and poor. The first waves of modern Jewish immigration (Aliyah) begin in 1881 due to pogroms in Eastern Europe.
  • 1897: Theodor Herzl convenes the First Zionist Congress in Basel, establishing modern political Zionism aimed at securing a Jewish homeland.
1917 - 1947 British Mandate & Road to Statehood
  • 1917 (Balfour Declaration): The British government issues a statement supporting the establishment of a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine.
  • 1920 - 1948: League of Nations grants Britain the Mandate over Palestine. Rising Jewish immigration leads to violent clashes between Arab and Jewish populations.
  • 1936 - 1939: The Arab Revolt in Palestine against British rule and Jewish immigration. British issue the White Paper of 1939, restricting Jewish entry right before the Holocaust.
  • 1947 (UN Partition Plan): UN Resolution 181 proposes dividing the land into independent Arab and Jewish states, with Jerusalem under international control. Accepted by Jewish leadership, rejected by Arab leadership.

State of Israel (1948 – Present)

1948 - 1949 Independence & The 1948 War
  • May 14, 1948: David Ben-Gurion declares the establishment of the State of Israel.
  • 1948 Arab-Israeli War: Surrounding Arab nations (Egypt, Syria, Jordan, Iraq, Lebanon) invade. Israel survives and expands its borders beyond the UN partition lines.
  • Results: Jordan occupies the West Bank and East Jerusalem; Egypt occupies Gaza. Roughly 700,000 Palestinian Arabs flee or are expelled (the Nakba), while an equal number of Jewish refugees flee or are expelled from Arab nations to Israel over subsequent years.
1956 - 1973 Decades of Conflict
  • 1956 (Suez Crisis): Israel invades the Sinai Peninsula in coordination with Britain and France after Egypt nationalizes the Suez Canal. Israel withdraws under US/Soviet pressure.
  • 1967 (Six-Day War): Following blockades and troop build-ups by Egypt, Israel launches preemptive strikes. In 6 days, Israel captures the West Bank, East Jerusalem, Gaza Strip, Golan Heights, and Sinai Peninsula.
  • 1973 (Yom Kippur War): Egypt and Syria launch a surprise attack on the holiest day of the Jewish calendar. Israel suffers heavy initial losses but pushes back forces. The war shatters Israel's aura of invincibility and leads to political shifts.
1979 - 1999 Peace Treaties & Intifada
  • 1979 (Egypt-Israel Peace): Menachem Begin and Anwar Sadat sign the Camp David Accords. Israel returns the Sinai to Egypt in exchange for full diplomatic relations.
  • 1982 (First Lebanon War): Israel invades Lebanon to stop PLO attacks, leading to a protracted occupation of southern Lebanon until 2000.
  • 1987 - 1993 (First Intifada): Palestinian uprising in the West Bank and Gaza. Leads to the creation of Hamas.
  • 1993 - 1995 (Oslo Accords): Yitzhak Rabin and Yasser Arafat sign agreements creating the Palestinian Authority (PA) with limited self-rule. In 1995, Rabin is assassinated by a Jewish extremist.
  • 1994: Israel signs a peace treaty with Jordan.
2000 - Present Modern Era Challenges & Accords
  • 2000 - 2005 (Second Intifada): Violent Palestinian uprising characterized by suicide bombings. Israel builds the West Bank barrier.
  • 2005: Israel unilaterally withdraws all troops and settlers from the Gaza Strip. Hamas takes control of Gaza in 2007, leading to a joint Israeli-Egyptian blockade.
  • 2006: Second Lebanon War fought between Israel and Hezbollah.
  • 2020 (Abraham Accords): US-brokered normalization agreements between Israel and the UAE, Bahrain, Morocco, and Sudan.
  • Oct 7, 2023 - Present: Hamas launches a massive surprise terrorist attack, killing ~1,200 Israelis and taking hostages. Triggers the ongoing Israel-Hamas war in Gaza, reshaping Middle East geopolitics.
