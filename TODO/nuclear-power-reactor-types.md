# Spec: Nuclear Power Reactor Types

**Target file:** `nuclear-power-reactor-types.html`

**Why this topic:** Nuclear discussions often collapse all reactors into one symbol. A useful cheatsheet should distinguish PWR, BWR, CANDU/PHWR, RBMK, gas-cooled, sodium fast, molten salt, HTGR, SMRs, and fusion-adjacent confusion by coolant, moderator, fuel, pressure, safety behavior, waste, deployment status, and what exists versus what is proposed.

## Content Approach

1. **Quick reference matrix:** reactor type, coolant, moderator, fuel, pressure, maturity, examples, strengths, failure modes.
2. **Core physics:** fission, chain reaction, moderator, control rods, coolant, decay heat, containment, neutron spectrum.
3. **Dominant light-water reactors:** PWR and BWR, why they dominate, operational differences, safety systems, examples.
4. **Heavy-water and graphite designs:** CANDU/PHWR, RBMK, AGR/Magnox, historical lessons and design distinctions.
5. **Advanced/future designs:** sodium fast, lead fast, molten salt, fluoride salt-cooled, HTGR, supercritical water, microreactors, SMRs. Be explicit about deployment maturity.
6. **Fuel cycle:** enriched uranium, natural uranium, MOX, thorium caveats, breeding, burnup, waste form.
7. **Safety comparison:** passive safety, negative temperature coefficients, coolant loss, pressure, chemical reactivity, containment, decay heat removal.
8. **Economics and deployment:** large reactors vs SMRs, factory learning claims, licensing, supply chain, construction risk.
9. **Common mistakes:** saying "nuclear" as one design, confusing molten salt coolant with thorium fuel, treating SMR as a physics type, ignoring decay heat, comparing proposed costs to built plants.

## Research Sources

Use IAEA reactor databases, NRC reactor technology pages, World Nuclear Association technical summaries, DOE/NEA resources, and official company/regulator filings for current SMR status. Verify all deployment counts and dates at build time.
