## Content Approach

1. **Quick reference:** concept, plain-English meaning, formula if useful, example, common misuse.
2. **Decision framing:** options, outcomes, probabilities, utilities, constraints, reversible vs irreversible decisions, one-way/two-way doors.
3. **Expected value:** money examples, health examples, time examples, nonlinear utility, risk aversion, tail risk.
4. **Base rates and calibration:** reference classes, inside/outside view, confidence intervals, prediction practice, Brier score.
5. **Value of information:** when to research more, sample, test, hire expert, run pilot; stopping rules.
6. **Decision tools:** decision trees, payoff matrices, premortems, checklists, weighted decision matrices, satisficing thresholds.
7. **Risk and uncertainty:** known probabilities vs ambiguity, robustness, minimax regret, option value, diversification.
8. **Social/game decisions:** incentives, commitment, signaling, coordination, principal-agent problems.
9. **Common mistakes:** false precision, ignoring opportunity cost, sunk cost, base-rate neglect, overresearching low-stakes choices, optimizing a proxy, averaging utilities across people casually, confusing probability with importance.
