# Spec: Comparative Religion Map

**Target file:** `comparative-religion-map.html`

**Why this topic:** Comparative religion pages often flatten traditions into trivia charts or become polemical. This page should be a respectful, structurally useful map: origins, texts, ultimate concerns, practices, authority, ritual life, ethics, afterlife/liberation views, internal diversity, and what outsiders commonly misunderstand.

## Targeting

- **Primary query:** "comparative religion chart". Research mode.
- **Secondary queries:** "world religions comparison", "Buddhism vs Christianity vs Islam", "religion family tree", "major religions beliefs chart", "Abrahamic Dharmic religions comparison".
- **Title:** `Comparative Religion Map: Traditions, Texts, Practices, and Beliefs`
- **H1:** `Comparative Religion Map`
- **Meta description:** "Compare major religious traditions respectfully: origins, texts, authority, practices, ethics, afterlife/liberation, internal branches, and common misunderstandings."
- **Reader outcome:** The reader can orient themselves among major traditions without reducing them to caricatures and can ask better follow-up questions.
- **Success metric:** Search traffic from comparison/chart queries and internal navigation to individual religion pages.
- **Volatile-facts register:** Historical/theological basics are STABLE. Demographic counts and contemporary institutional facts are SLOW-DRIFT; verify from Pew/official/statistical sources if included. Overall: STABLE/SLOW-DRIFT.
- **Index category:** Philosophy & Religion.
- **Jurisdiction scope:** Global, with humility about internal diversity and regional variation.
- **Reading conditions:** Desktop/tablet study mode; some school/research use. Prioritize maps, comparison tables, and caveats that prevent flattening.
- **Cross-link map:** Outbound to `buddhism.html`, `buddhism-core-principles.html`, `judaism.html`, `islam.html`, `shabbat-services-cheatsheet.html`, `etz-chaim-tree-of-life.html`, `objectivism.html`, `capitalism.html`.

## Content Approach

1. **Quick reference chart:** tradition, family, origin, major texts, central concern, practice mode, authority structure, liberation/salvation/ultimate aim, major branches.
2. **Map of families:** Abrahamic, Dharmic, East Asian, Indigenous/traditional, new religious movements, nonreligious philosophies. Avoid ranking.
3. **Comparison dimensions:** God/ultimate reality, human problem, path/practice, text/canon, ritual/community, ethics, afterlife/liberation, authority, conversion/identity.
4. **Tradition profiles:** Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Hindu traditions, Buddhism, Sikhism, Jainism, Taoism/Daoism, Confucianism, Shinto, Indigenous/traditional religions, secular humanism/nonreligious worldviews if handled carefully.
5. **Internal diversity:** branches and why single labels hide major differences.
6. **Historical relationships:** shared roots, influence, divergence, syncretism, colonization/modernity effects.
7. **How to compare without caricature:** emic/etic distinction, practice vs doctrine, elite texts vs lived religion, demographic vs theological claims.
8. **Common mistakes:** treating religions as belief checklists, confusing ethnicity/nationality/religion, using one sect as the whole, flattening Hinduism/Buddhism/Indigenous traditions, assuming all religions answer the same question.

## Research Sources

Use Pew Research for demographics, Oxford/Cambridge/academic references, primary tradition sources for basic terms where possible, existing site pages for internal consistency, and encyclopedia-level academic sources for neutral summaries. Verify demographics at build time.

## Visual Design

**Museum wall map aesthetic:** neutral cartographic palette with tradition family branches and comparison lenses. Signature element and og:image: an interactive "religion family + comparison lens" map, not a winner/loser chart. Use respectful restrained colors and clear caveat badges.
