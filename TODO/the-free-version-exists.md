# Spec: The Free Version Exists — Paid Services With Hidden Free Equivalents

**Target file:** `the-free-version-exists.html`

**Why this topic:** A whole class of businesses exists *because* a free equivalent is deliberately obscured — TurboTax lobbied for years against free filing while hiding its own Free File edition from search engines (the FTC settlement is public record), credit-monitoring subscriptions sell what credit freezes do better for $0, and third-party sites charge $40 to file forms governments provide free. No commercial site can publish this directory because every entry destroys an affiliate category. It's the purest aggregation play in the collection: fifty scattered facts, each individually known, never compiled.

## Content approach

Every entry in identical four-field anatomy, ruthlessly compact: **The paid thing** (typical cost/yr) → **The free version** (name + official URL) → **Where it hides** (why you haven't used it — the dark pattern, the lobbying, the sponsored-result trick) → **The catch** (honest: what you give up, and when the paid version actually wins). The fourth field is the credibility engine — a directory that admits when paid wins gets believed about the rest.

Opening block: **the pattern** (~200 words + the three tells): (1) a sponsored result sits above an official .gov (the imposter-intermediary tell), (2) the paid product *monitors* what a free tool *prevents*, (3) the "convenience fee" is for typing your data into the free form. Teach the reflex: before paying for anything government-adjacent, search "<thing> site:.gov" first.

Entries grouped in six sections; density target: **45–55 entries total**, each 3–5 lines. Rough allocation:

1. **Money & credit (10–12):** credit reports (AnnualCreditReport.com — now weekly, the only federally authorized site) vs. paid report sites; **credit monitoring subscriptions vs. freezes at all three bureaus** (the flagship entry — freeze prevents, monitoring narrates; $0 vs. $120–360/yr); free FICO via card issuers vs. score subscriptions; **IRS Direct File + Free File vs. paid tax prep** (the TurboTax FTC settlement told in three lines; income thresholds dated) + VITA/TCE free preparation; state unclaimed-property databases (+ missingmoney.com) vs. "finders" charging 10–35% cuts; TreasuryDirect I-bonds/savings bonds vs. broker-wrapped equivalents; CFPB complaint vs. paid "credit repair" (what repair firms legally can do = what you can do free — dispute letters); HUD-approved housing counselors vs. paid foreclosure "rescue"; annual Social Security statement (ssa.gov) vs. benefit "analysis" services; FAFSA vs. paid FAFSA-filing services (it's the *Free* Application — people pay anyway); bank fee-waiver scripts vs. overdraft "protection" products.
2. **Government & legal (8–10):** **EIN filing (IRS: $0, 10 minutes) vs. $40–300 filing-service sites** (the archetype entry); passport forms/appointments (travel.state.gov) vs. expediter *sites* (honest catch: legitimate expediters exist for genuine rush — the scam is paying for the form); DMV official sites vs. third-party lookalikes (the ad-above-the-.gov pattern); court self-help centers + fee waivers (in forma pauperis exists — link small-claims); state statutory will/POA/AHCD forms vs. template subscriptions (link estate-documents); FOIA/public records vs. paid background-check aggregators (which resell public records); federal trademark/copyright basics: USPTO/copyright.gov direct vs. filing-mill markups (catch: real attorneys add real value in disputes — say so); voter registration, birth/death certificates from the actual vital-records office vs. reseller sites; free notaries (your bank, credit union, library) vs. UPS-store fees.
3. **Health (7–9):** insurance nurse lines (back of every card) + 988 + Poison Control (1-800-222-1222) vs. panic urgent-care visits (link er-triage); pharmacy cash/discount reality — manufacturer copay cards and patient-assistance programs vs. paying list; **hospital charity care — legally required at nonprofit hospitals** vs. paying the first bill (link the future medical-bills content and death-logistics' cousin logic); free annual wellness visit / preventive services mandated under ACA (dated); PubMed/MedlinePlus vs. symptom-content mills; state/county health department clinics (vaccines, STI testing — often free) vs. retail pricing; dental schools + federally qualified health centers' sliding scales; SAM.gov-adjacent: NIH clinical trials as care access (honest catch included); glasses Rx release rights (link eyeglasses page).
4. **Learning & entertainment (7–9):** **the library super-entry** (Libby/OverDrive, Hoopla, Kanopy, Freegal, LinkedIn Learning via many systems, museum passes, "library of things" tool lending, meeting rooms — priced against the subscriptions it replaces: honestly $50–150/mo of equivalents); Internet Archive + Project Gutenberg + Standard Ebooks vs. public-domain paperback resellers ($14.99 print-on-demand copies of free texts — a genuine scam-adjacent category); open courseware (MIT OCW, Khan, Coursera-audit-mode's hidden free tier) vs. course-platform upsells; museum free days/Bank of America Museums on Us-class programs (card-class level); IRS-adjacent: FreeTaxUSA-style freemium honesty note; state parks library passes; Duolingo-class freemium vs. the library's Mango/Rosetta access.
5. **Consumer & travel (7–9):** **credit-card extended warranty (free automatic doubling on most cards) vs. purchased extended warranties** (the insurance-worth-it crossover flagship); card trip-delay/rental-CDW benefits vs. counter insurance (link flight-disruption + insurance); the airline 24-hour free cancellation rule (DOT) vs. "cancellation protection" upsells; DOT/EU261 cash rights vs. claim-firm 30% cuts (link flight-disruption); Magnuson-Moss + implied-warranty rights vs. "protection plans" (the void-sticker myth in one line); manufacturer goodwill/secret warranties (TSBs — link the auto page) vs. paying out of warranty; price-drop/return-window policies vs. "price protection" apps' premium tiers; free credit-card rental car coverage decision (honest catch: exotic/luxury/Ireland exclusions); hotel/airline direct booking price-match programs vs. OTA "deals."
6. **Tech & everyday (6–8):** the open-source swap table, kept to the paid-default pattern per Rule 4 (LibreOffice vs. Office for home basics, GIMP/Krita, Bitwarden free tier vs. password-manager subscriptions, VLC, 7-Zip vs. WinRAR's eternal trial — each one line with the honest catch: collaboration/compatibility edges where paid wins); OS-built-ins vs. paid utilities (PDF signing in Preview/Edge, screen recording, disk cleanup vs. "cleaner" apps — which are scam-adjacent; link security pages); Google Voice-class free second lines vs. paid burner apps; free tiers that genuinely suffice (email aliasing, cloud storage math); Wi-Fi calling vs. signal-booster purchases (honest catch included); manufacturer manuals/parts diagrams free online vs. manual-reseller sites.

Closing block: **when paid genuinely wins** — a short honest list so the page isn't dogma: real CPAs for complex returns, real attorneys for contested matters, real expediters for real deadlines, paid password managers for family sharing, convenience as a legitimate purchase when the free path costs hours you value more. One line each; this section is what separates the page from a rant.

**Common mistakes** section: paying the sponsored result (the .gov reflex), assuming free = scam (the inversion this page exists to fix), signing up for the "free trial" version of a thing that has an actually-free official version, using a finder service that takes a cut of *your own* unclaimed money, and not checking the card benefits you already pay an annual fee for.

Research: every entry verified against its official source URL (that URL is part of the entry); the FTC-TurboTax settlement documents; CFPB/FTC pages for the credit entries; Rule 1 applies with force — an entry with an unverifiable free path gets cut, not hedged.

## Visual design

**Receipt-and-tag aesthetic** — the page as a long store receipt where every line item gets refunded: struck-through prices, $0 tags, and a running savings total.

- Palette: receipt-paper white/off-white with faint thermal-printer gray rules, near-black monospace-leaning type for prices, **refund green** for every $0 tag and the tally, red strikethrough for the paid prices. One accent, used everywhere the money is.
- **The signature element and og:image: the running savings tally** — a sticky footer strip (mobile) / side rail (desktop) that sums "annual savings so far" as the reader scrolls past entries (IntersectionObserver, plain JS; static grand-total fallback without JS). The og:image is the receipt motif with the grand total torn off at the bottom — "$X,XXX/yr, itemized inside."
- **Entries as receipt line items:** paid thing + struck price on the left, the free equivalent + green $0 on the right, the where-it-hides and the-catch lines beneath in smaller type. Category sections divided by perforated-tear rules.
- Category jump-nav as receipt department headers (GROCERY-style all-caps: MONEY / LEGAL / HEALTH / LEARNING / TRAVEL / TECH), sticky-top on mobile with the in-page filter box.
- The three tells (§ opening) as a bordered "CASHIER NOTICE" card — the one instructional block, styled as a taped-up register sign.
- The when-paid-wins section visually inverted: clean white card, no strikethroughs, a small "worth it" tag in plain black — the design admits the exceptions the way the copy does.
- Every official URL rendered visibly (not just linked) — on this page the URL *is* the content; monospace, green, copy-friendly.
