# Spec: The Household Numbers — The Right Setting for Everything (Not the Factory Default)

**Target file:** `the-household-numbers.html`

**Why this topic:** Every device and system in a home ships with a default chosen for the *manufacturer's* interests — liability, energy-label compliance, showroom impression, return-rate minimization — not the occupant's. The evidence-based setpoints (water heater temperature, fridge/freezer temps, humidity bands, tire pressure, HVAC settings, router placement) exist scattered across DOE fact sheets, CDC/OSHA guidance, and engineering references; nobody normalizes them into one page because no seller profits from you adjusting a dial for free. The user's framing is the thesis: **real numbers, not manufacturer defaults** — and the delta between them is measurable money, safety, and comfort.

## Content approach

The recurring anatomy is the page's engine — every entry runs the same five fields: **The number** (big, first) / **The default & why it's wrong for you** (what the factory optimized: liability, energy label, showroom) / **The evidence** (agency/physics, cited) / **The tradeoff** (what moving the dial costs — every number is a tradeoff honestly stated) / **How to verify** (because dials lie: fridge dials are unnumbered, water-heater dials are approximate — the $15 instrument list is part of the page). Entries grouped by room/system; density target: **35–45 numbers total.**

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the settings card.** The whole house on one card — number, location, verify-with — designed as the printable. ~20 headline values: water heater 120°F (or 140 + mixing valve), fridge 35–38°F, freezer 0°F, winter humidity 30–40%, summer <50–60%, bedroom sleep temp ~65–68°F, thermostat setback ~7–10°F when away, tire PSI = door-jamb placard, dishwasher water inlet ≥120°F, dryer... etc. (final list = the build's §2–7 headliners).
2. **Water systems.** The **water-heater centerpiece entry** — the page's thesis in miniature: shipped hot for legionella-liability, DOE says 120°F (scald: 5-min burn at 120 vs. ~5 sec at 140 — the exposure table; child/elder skin faster), the legionella counterpoint (ASHRAE/OSHA: growth range peaks ~95–115°F; immunocompromised households should run 140 storage + **thermostatic mixing valve** delivering 120 — the both-sides resolution the SERP never gives), plus $/yr per 10°F as a formula with a dated example; how to actually measure (tap thermometer after a heavy-use hour) and where the real dial is (electric: behind panels, breaker off). Supporting entries: softener hardness setting (test first — link air-water), pressure regulator 40–60 PSI (above 80 eats fixtures; the $10 gauge on a hose bib), pipe-freeze threshold folklore (drip faucets below ~20°F exterior-wall reality), sump pump test cadence, water main location (not a number — the one "know where" entry that earns its place).
3. **Kitchen cold & heat.** Fridge **35–38°F** and freezer **0°F** (defaults/dials optimized for energy labels and noise complaints; the FDA 40°F ceiling vs. the practical 37 target; the unnumbered-dial problem → $8 appliance thermometer, 24-hr check, door vs. back-wall placement of the thermometer), fridge-door storage truth (the door is the warmest zone — milk doesn't belong there: the one placement folklore entry), freezer-burn is a quality-not-safety number, dishwasher: inlet water ≥120°F and skip pre-rinsing (detergent enzymes need soil — the counterintuitive one, cited to detergent-maker and consumer-lab testing), fridge coil-cleaning cadence, oven true-temp check ($10 oven thermometer; factory calibration drifts ±25°F+ — and the calibration-offset menu most ovens hide), meat safe temps in one line + link to the future kitchen tables page (Rule 4: don't inline a second cheatsheet).
4. **Air & climate.** Winter RH 30–40% (below 30: static, nosebleeds, wood cracks; above 40 in cold climates: window condensation → mold — the climate-dependent honesty), summer <50–60% (dust mites/mold thresholds), the $12 hygrometer; sleep temp ~65–68°F (evidence summarized, link sleep-optimization); thermostat setbacks (the "reheating costs more" myth killed with the DOE numbers; heat-pump exception honestly flagged — setbacks work differently with heat pumps), HVAC filter: MERV 8–13 sweet spot and the check-monthly-change-when-gray rule (calendar intervals are marketing; link air-water for depth), CO2 <~1,000 ppm as the ventilation proxy, ceiling-fan direction by season (the switch nobody flips), radon: EPA 4 pCi/L action level + the $15 test as the entry point (the one test-not-setting entry that stays — it's the #1 number nobody knows).
5. **Safety numbers.** Smoke alarm: test monthly, replace unit at 10 yrs (the date-on-the-back check — alarms expire, the least-known number on the page); CO alarm: replace 5–7 yrs, alarm thresholds explained (UL 2034 tolerates low-level chronic CO — why "it never went off" isn't "there's no CO"; the low-level-monitor upgrade for the vulnerable); fire extinguisher: ABC, pressure-gauge check, 10–12 yr life; dryer vent cleaning cadence (lint fires); garage-door auto-reverse test (the 2×4 test); water leak: the $12 sensor placement list; gun storage numbers link safes page (one line); night light/pathway lux for elders (falls — link actual-risk-dashboard).
6. **Garage & outdoors.** Tire PSI: **door-jamb placard, never the sidewall max** (the entry that corrects the internet's most durable car confusion; cold-inflation meaning, ~1 PSI per 10°F seasonal drift, TPMS warns ~25% low — far too late, the $15 gauge habit), engine oil on the dipstick monthly (link auto-repair-decoder for intervals), garage fridge winter problem (single-thermostat units quit below ~50°F ambient — the folklore entry), lawn mowing height by grass goal (taller = fewer weeds), hose-bib winterization threshold, deck/ladder load realities in one line, car battery: 12.6 V rested = healthy, <12.4 = charge (the $15 multimeter's first job), grill safe-distance number.
7. **Digital & electrical.** Router placement beats router settings (central + elevated; the 2.4/5 GHz one-liner), UPS runtime honesty, surge protector joule ratings and their expiry (protection wears out — the replace-after-big-events folklore), breaker ampacity is not a target (the 80% continuous-load rule in one line, "call an electrician" boundary per Rule 4), screen brightness/night settings link sleep page, backup cadence: the 3-2-1 line (link personal-cybersecurity), monitor ergonomics: top at eye level (the one desk number).
8. **Common mistakes:** trusting unnumbered dials without a thermometer, setting the sidewall PSI, "saving money" at 130°F fridge... (i.e., over-warm fridge to cut energy — the food-loss math loses), humidity chased to 45% in a −10°F January (condensation), replacing alarm batteries but never the expired alarms, MERV-16 filters choking a residential blower (higher isn't better — static pressure honesty), setback myths on heat pumps, pre-rinsing dishes, and adjusting everything once but never re-verifying (annual walk-through: the page's closing ritual, tied to the printable card).

Research: DOE/Energy Saver pages (water heat, setbacks), FDA/USDA (cold chain), EPA (radon, humidity/mold), CPSC (alarms, garage doors), ASHRAE/OSHA (legionella position), NFPA (extinguishers, dryer fires), tire-industry placard guidance. Rule 1: every number above is an anchor; the build cites each entry's evidence field to its primary source, and where authorities genuinely disagree (water heater), the page presents both with dates — that honesty is the brand.

## Visual design

**Control-panel aesthetic** — the house rendered as one big instrument panel: every number lives in a gauge, dial, or seven-segment readout set to the correct value.

- Palette: instrument-panel charcoal-slate panels on a light workshop-wall background (the page alternates: guidance prose on light, number-displays as dark inset panels), with **correct-value green** for the target numbers, amber for tradeoff/range bounds, and the factory default shown in a dim strikethrough gray on each gauge — *default vs. right value* rendered as two needles on every dial. That two-needle motif IS the thesis made visual.
- **Signature element & og:image: the settings card as an instrument cluster** — the ~20 headline numbers as a grid of mini-gauges/readouts (thermometer arcs, PSI dial, RH% arc, 7-segment 0°F), each labeled, each showing the dim default needle and the green target needle. The og:image is a 6–8 gauge crop. The print stylesheet renders the same card flat (numbers + labels, no dark panels) for the fridge door.
- **Entry anatomy as a panel row:** the big number left (seven-segment or dial styling per type), the five anatomy fields right in compact labeled rows (DEFAULT / EVIDENCE / TRADEOFF / VERIFY) — machine-placard typography (engraved-label small caps, echoing but lighter than the safes page's plates: consumer panel vs. industrial vault).
- The **verify-with instrument list** as a small "toolbox" card early on (appliance thermometer, hygrometer, tire gauge, outlet/multimeter, radon kit — ~$60 total; prices dated) — the page's one shopping list, generic per Rule 4.
- The scald/time exposure table and the humidity climate bands as small horizontal band charts (temperature/RH axis with colored zones) — consistent zone-band language across entries.
- Room/system section headers as breaker-panel labels (the directory card inside an electrical panel door — handwritten-label aesthetic, one per section).
- All gauges are decorative SVG with the number in real text (a11y + SEO); 375-px: panel rows stack, gauges shrink to badge size, the number never shrinks.
