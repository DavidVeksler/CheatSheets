# Spec: The Parent's Threat Model — What Actually Endangers Kids

**Target file:** `parents-threat-model.html`

**Why this topic:** Parental fear is allocated by salience, not statistics — stranger abduction, vans, Halloween candy — while the actual harm distribution (vehicles, drowning, unsecured firearms in visited homes, the 30-second silent bathtub) gets a fraction of the vigilance. No parenting-industry author can publish the honest ranking because "worry less about strangers, ask about the gun before the playdate" alienates advertisers and readers in equal measure; the aggregate data (CDC WISQARS, injury-epidemiology literature) is exactly LLM-shaped. This is the actual-risk-dashboard method applied at maximum emotional leverage — and its most useful output is *scripts*: the awkward questions that address the real top of the list.

## Content approach

Tone rules stated to the builder, because this page fails if the tone fails: (1) never mock a parental fear — salience bias is human, and the page reframes rather than ridicules ("your brain is doing what brains do; here's the recalibration"); (2) the payoff of every downgraded fear is an *upgraded* one — worry is redirected, not dismissed; (3) the firearm section is written as safety practice with zero policy valence — storage and asking, nothing else; (4) grief-adjacent statistics get plain, respectful language, no cleverness. The page's spine: **vigilance is a budget; spend it where the deaths are.**

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the two-list graphic.** The feared list vs. the actual list, side by side with annual US death/injury scale per item: FEARED — stranger abduction (~100–150 stereotypical stranger kidnappings/yr from a population of 70M+ children), poisoned Halloween candy (~zero documented cases ever — the Best research), vans, school-shooter odds as perceived vs. actual (handled with care and honest numbers), plane crashes; ACTUAL — motor vehicles (passenger + pedestrian + driveway), drowning (#1 killer of 1–4s), firearms in the home environment (the crossover fact for 1–19, dated), suffocation (infants), falls, poisoning (meds/edibles — the modern riser). Every number sourced and dated; the asymmetry is the whole argument and it must survive fact-checking.
2. **The ranking by age band — the page's spine.** Four bands (0–1 / 1–4 / 5–12 / 13–19), each with: the top-5 causes from WISQARS, the near-miss layer (leading ER-visit causes — the nonfatal picture differs and matters), and the **three highest-leverage interventions for that band** with effect evidence: 0–1: safe sleep (link baby-gear's section, don't duplicate), rear-facing seat correctly installed, water (bath) rules; 1–4: **drowning band** — barriers/fences (the four-sided-fence evidence), touch supervision, the driveway/backover pattern; 5–12: pedestrian/bike (helmets, crossing), firearm storage at *other* houses, med storage; 13–19: the band where the graph changes shape — driving (the GDL-practices entry: passenger limits and night rules are the evidence-backed parental levers), suicide means restriction (handled with the same means-matter evidence as the safes/storage sections, plain and brief, 988 present), overdose/fentanyl reality (real, unlike the candy myth — the honest contrast the page is built for). Density: 4 bands × (5 causes + 3 interventions) fully populated.
3. **The deep dives on the big four.** One block each, mechanism → the folklore correction → the checklist:
   - **Vehicles:** correct restraint use by stage (the misuse-rate stat — most seats are installed or used wrong; the inspection-station/CPST fix), rear-facing duration, the hot-car pattern (routine-change physiology, not "bad parents" — the reminder habits), driveways and backovers, teen GDL practices.
   - **Water:** drowning is silent and fast (the 30-second reality vs. the splashing-movie version), the **Water Watcher protocol** (a designated, phone-down adult with a physical token — the page's most shareable card), pool fencing law-vs-practice, life jackets vs. floaties (the inflatable-toy false-security note), bathtub rules, open-water cold/current notes, swim lessons evidence by age (AAP position, dated).
   - **Firearms:** the exposure math (visited-homes probability — why "we don't own one" doesn't finish the job), safe storage practice (locked + unloaded + ammo separate; quick-access safes as the compatible-with-defense option — link safes page), and **the asking scripts** (see §4). Policy-free by construction.
   - **Poisoning, modern edition:** meds (grandparents' pill organizers — the classic vector), cannabis edibles (the look-alike packaging data, dated), button batteries (the honey interim + ER-now protocol; the 2022 Reese's Law fix, dated), laundry pods, Poison Control 1-800-222-1222 saved in the phone (link er-triage).
4. **The awkward-questions section — the scripts.** The page's signature utility; each script as a copy-ready card with the normalization framing ("asked like allergies, answered like allergies"): the gun question ("Before the kids play — is there an unsecured gun in the house? We ask everyone."), the pool question, the driving question for teen carpools (who's driving, how many kids, night?), the allergy exchange (both directions), the water-watcher recruitment line at parties, and the reciprocal-disclosure etiquette (offer your answers first — the folklore that makes asking easy). Plus the awkwardness reframe: the one-line stat that pediatricians and the AAP formally recommend the gun/pool questions — you're not the paranoid one, you're following the guideline.
5. **The fears you can retire — with receipts.** The salience-bias graveyard, each in three lines (the fear / the actual base rate with source / where that worry-hour goes instead): Halloween sadism (Best's decades-null research), stereotypical stranger abduction vs. the real abduction picture (overwhelmingly custody/known-adult — and the actual online-grooming risk that replaces it, honestly sized), "kids can't play outside anymore" (crime-trend data vs. perception — the independence paragraph, with the honest developmental cost of over-restriction cited), quicksand-class movie fears, razor blades/apples, van abductions. The section closes with the redirect table: each retired fear mapped to its upgraded replacement (candy → med storage; vans → the parking-lot/driveway 10 minutes; strangers → the water watcher).
6. **The near-misses that fill ERs.** The nonfatal top-10 (falls dominate — playground surfacing, window guards vs. screens, furniture/TV tip-overs and the anchor kit, trampolines honestly (the AAP position vs. the netting-marketing), bunk beds, bikes/scooters, sports concussion basics, dog bites (the known-dog reality), burns (stovetop geography, the coffee-cup radius) — each: one stat + one fix. Density: 10 entries. Link er-triage as the "when it happens" companion.
7. **Common mistakes:** vigilance spent on strangers while the pool gate stands open, "we taught him not to touch it" as a firearm plan (the curiosity studies — education doesn't survive contact with an actual gun; storage does), floaties as supervision, treating the driveway as not-a-road, med bottles as child-resistant-equals-child-proof (it's a delay, not a barrier), asking no questions before the sleepover while grilling the school about lockdown drills, and letting this page make you *more* anxious — the closing paragraph returns to the budget frame: childhood deaths are historically rare and falling (the good-news base rate, cited); the page exists to aim vigilance, not enlarge it.

Research: CDC WISQARS fatal + nonfatal queries (primary; rebuild tables from current data at build time), AAP policy statements (car seats, drowning, firearms-in-home guidance, trampolines, swim lessons), NHTSA (restraint misuse, hot cars, backovers), Joel Best's Halloween research, NCMEC abduction typology, Safe Kids Worldwide. Rule 1 at full force — this page will be adversarially fact-checked by upset readers from every direction; every number carries its source and year inline.

## Visual design

**Attention-budget aesthetic** — a calm daylight infographic-poster language where *area = risk*, so the eye learns the proportions before reading a word. Warm but grown-up: picture-book palette discipline without any childishness.

- Palette: warm paper white, deep slate ink, and a two-hue system carried everywhere: **sun-amber for real risks** (attention goes here) and a **cool fog-blue for feared-but-rare** (let these cool off) — the redirect motif in color. Rounded-rectangle geometry, generous whitespace, zero clip-art, no children silhouettes in peril (design taboo — the page shows proportions, never depicted harm).
- **Signature element & og:image: the paired proportional-area treemap** — FEARED (fog-blue blocks sized by *search/media volume*) beside ACTUAL (amber blocks sized by real deaths/injuries): the van block huge in panel one and invisible in panel two; drowning the reverse. Built as one SVG, og:image is the pair with the title strap.
- **Age-band sections as a growth-ruler spine:** a vertical height-ruler motif runs the page's left rail with the four bands marked; each band's top-causes as a small horizontal bar set (amber), interventions as three numbered chips.
- **The scripts as speech-bubble cards:** large-type quoted scripts in rounded bubbles with a copy button and a "asked like allergies" caption line — sized for screenshot (the group-chat artifact); print stylesheet renders the script set + water-watcher card on one page.
- The **Water Watcher card** as a literal badge/lanyard design (the physical-token protocol drawn as a wearable tag) — printable at badge size, the page's second physical artifact.
- Retired fears rendered as fog-blue cards that visually "deflate" (muted, smaller type) with the receipt line beneath, each paired by an arrow to its amber replacement — the redirect table as a visual rhyme.
- The near-miss section as a compact pictogram grid (ISO-style flat glyphs: window, TV, trampoline, dog) with stat + fix lines.
- Mobile: treemap pair stacks vertically with areas preserved; ruler rail collapses to inline band headers; scripts stay full-width cards. All charts real-text-labeled for a11y/SEO.
