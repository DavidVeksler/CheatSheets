# Spec: Death Logistics — The First 72 Hours & the Funeral Industry's Playbook

**Target file:** `death-logistics.html`

**Why this topic:** Everyone needs this exactly once, at the worst possible moment to do research, and the existing information environment is purely adversarial — nearly every guide online is written by a funeral home or lead-gen site. No credentialed expert puts their name on honest funeral-pricing content. An LLM with no affiliate links can write the guide that commercially cannot exist.

## Content approach

Tone: calm, practical, consumer-protection. This is a checklist-and-rights page, not a grief page (one short empathetic line at top, then all business).

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the first 72 hours checklist.** Ordered, time-boxed: pronouncement of death (who can do it: hospital vs. hospice vs. home → call 911 vs. hospice nurse), do-NOT-rush notice (the body does not need to go anywhere within hours; you may take time), organ/tissue donation window, notifying immediate family, securing the home/pets/valuables, finding the will / prepaid arrangements BEFORE calling a funeral home, obtaining 10–15 certified death certificates (why so many, typical cost $10–25 each), who to notify in week one vs. month one (SSA, employer, banks, insurers, credit bureaus — flag deceased to prevent identity theft — DMV, USPS). Density target: 25–35 time-boxed checklist items across the four time bands; the notification list alone should run 12–15 entities.
2. **Your FTC Funeral Rule rights.** Itemized price list (GPL) must be given over the phone and in writing; you may buy a casket anywhere (Costco/Amazon, and the funeral home cannot refuse it or charge a handling fee); embalming is almost never legally required (list the actual exceptions); you can decline packages and buy à la carte. Note the Funeral Rule's 2024+ proposed update to require online price posting — verify current status. Density target: 6–8 distinct rights, one card each, every card carrying its citation.
3. **Disposition options with real prices.** Comparison table: direct cremation (~$1,000–2,500), full-service burial (national median ~$8,000–10,000 + plot + vault), green/natural burial, alkaline hydrolysis (aqua cremation — legal-state list), human composting (NOR — legal-state list, ~$5,000–7,000), body donation to science ($0, what actually happens, reputable programs vs. body-broker scandals), burial at sea, home funeral (legal in most states). Columns: typical cost, timeline, legality/availability, gotchas. Density target: 8–9 rows × 4 columns, every cost cell a real dated number — no "varies."
4. **The upsell playbook, decoded.** The specific tactics: "protective" sealed caskets (do nothing, ~$1,000+ upcharge), casket showroom ordering (cheap ones hidden), embalming presented as required, "cremation casket" when a cardboard container is fine, package bundling, guilt framing ("what your mother would have wanted"). For each: the counter-script — literal sentences to say. Density target: the six tactics here are the floor; each ships with its counter-script, no orphaned tactics.
5. **Estate quick-start (first month).** Not a full probate guide: locate will, file with probate court (deadline varies by state), small-estate affidavit thresholds exist, what NOT to pay from your own pocket (the estate's debts are generally not the family's — forced-collection scripts), beneficiary-designated assets bypass probate, when you actually need a lawyer.
6. **Pre-need planning block** (for the reader doing this ahead of time): why pre-PLANNING beats pre-PAYING (prepaid plan failure modes), the one-page "when I die" letter, POLST/advance directive vs. will.
7. **Common mistakes:** calling a funeral home before checking for prepaid plans, paying deceased's debts personally, buying at the showroom, ordering too few death certificates, rushing decisions in the first 24h.

Research: FTC Funeral Rule text, NFDA median price surveys (dated), state legality lists for NOR/alkaline hydrolysis (volatile — date them), Funeral Consumers Alliance.

## Visual design

Distinct feel: **calm document / field-manual hybrid** — warm off-white or pale paper background, restrained serif headings, generous whitespace. Deliberately the visual opposite of the dark data-dashboard pages; it should feel like a steady hand on the shoulder.

- **Timeline spine for section 1:** a vertical left-rail timeline (First hour / First day / First week / First month) with checkbox-styled items (real `<input type=checkbox>` — people will use it live; persist state to localStorage).
- **Rights (section 2) as "rights cards":** bordered cards each stating one right in large text with the legal citation small beneath — designed to be screenshot-able / shown on a phone inside a funeral home.
- **Disposition table** with a subtle cost-scale bar in each row.
- **Upsell playbook as paired script blocks:** "They say" (italic, gray) / "You say" (bold, quoted) — visually like dialogue.
- A muted accent color (sage or slate blue), zero playful iconography.
- **og:image / shareable artifact:** the 1200×630 social card is a single rights card — the casket right ("You may buy a casket anywhere; the funeral home cannot refuse it or charge a handling fee," citation small beneath) in the card's large-type styling, which survives thumbnailing. The "screenshot this" artifact is the rights-card block as a whole — it's already designed to be shown on a phone inside a funeral home, so the share card and the signature element stay one design.
