# SEO audit — cheatsheets.davidveksler.com

**Date:** 2026-07-09 · **Data:** Google Search Console, 180-day window 2026-01-10 → 2026-07-08
(the 28-day snapshot in [`seo-planning.md`](seo-planning.md) is too short to see the real shape).
**Corpus:** 151 root `.html` files.

Companion doc: [`seo-implementation-spec.md`](seo-implementation-spec.md) — the executable spec derived
from this audit.

---

## Method note: three false positives, and why the numbers below are trustworthy

A first pass with naïve regexes reported 68 pages missing `meta description`, 67 missing `canonical`,
64 missing `og:image`, and 5 with invalid JSON-LD. **All four were artifacts, not defects:**

| Apparent defect | Actual cause |
|---|---|
| Missing description / canonical / og:image | Files use `content="…" name="description"` (reversed attribute order, from an HTML-tidy pass). A `name=…` -first regex misses them. |
| Invalid JSON-LD (5 files) | Those files have **two or three** `ld+json` blocks; the parser concatenated them into one invalid string. Each block parses fine individually. |
| Titles > 60 chars (90 files) | Inline **SVG `<title>`** elements were being captured as the document title, inflating lengths. |

Every finding below was re-derived with a real HTML parser scoped to `<head>`, and the counts were
spot-checked against the raw files. Where a number matters, it was verified twice.

## 1. The headline finding: impressions are enormous, clicks are not

| Page | Impressions | Clicks | CTR | Avg. pos |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `ai-frontier.html` | 271,765 | 584 | 0.215% | 8.1 |
| `orbital-rockets-comparison.html` | 155,773 | 382 | 0.245% | 7.8 |
| `tesla-products.html` | 92,547 | 89 | 0.096% | 8.4 |
| `humanoid-robots.html` | 71,298 | 15 | **0.021%** | 8.9 |
| `javascript-for-architects.html` | 50,598 | 8 | 0.016% | 20.4 |
| `google-ai-studio-guide.html` | 43,759 | 164 | 0.375% | 7.6 |
| `postgresql.html` | 40,185 | 35 | 0.087% | 13.5 |
| `martial-arts-cheatsheet.html` | 37,881 | 45 | 0.119% | 7.5 |
| `bitcoin-whitepaper.html` | 23,208 | **2** | 0.009% | 9.4 |

A page at average position 8 should earn roughly **2–3% CTR**. These pages earn 0.02–0.4% — between
10× and 250× below expectation. That gap is the whole story, and it has **two distinct causes that
must not be conflated**, because only one of them is fixable by editing HTML.

### Cause A — SERP-feature absorption (mostly *not* fixable on-page)

The clearest case is `judo.html`: the single query **"judo guide" produced 17,854 impressions and 2
clicks at average position 7.0** — 59% of that page's total impressions, converting at 0.011%. A
genuine 7th-position blue link for a query with that volume would return hundreds of clicks.

Corroborating signal: GSC reports four **anchor-fragment URLs** for the flagship page —
`ai-frontier.html#title-openai`, `#title-anthropic`, `#title-deepmind`, `#title-xai` — each with
**3,281 impressions and 0 clicks** at position 7.1. Those are Google-generated "jump to section"
links, which appear inside SERP features, not as standalone results.

The consistent read: a large share of this site's impressions come from **AI Overviews and other
SERP features** that log an impression and satisfy the user without a click. This site is
informational reference content under clear headings — precisely the shape AI Overviews extract from.

**Implication:** do not set a site-wide CTR target and do not judge these pages as "failing." The
impressions are real reach (goal-4 advocacy, goal-3 case study), and being the *cited source* in an
AI Overview has value that GSC does not measure. Chasing blue-link CTR here will disappoint.

### Cause B — the listing itself is broken (fixable, and worth doing)

Independent of AI Overviews, three mechanical defects suppress clicks on every query the site ranks
for. These are unambiguous and cheap to fix:

1. **82 of 151 titles exceed 60 characters** and are truncated in the SERP; 30 exceed 70.
   The worst are 99, 96, 92, and 90 characters. Filler is the cause — `Ultimate Guide to`,
   `(Printable & Trackable)`, `Interactive`, `Comprehensive`, `Enhanced`, `| David Veksler Cheatsheets`.
2. **52 descriptions exceed 200 characters**; Google truncates or discards and writes its own.
3. **Titles do not use the words people actually search.** This is the single most valuable finding.

The third point deserves its own section.

## 2. The flagship page's title does not contain its own queries

`ai-frontier.html` earns 47% of all site clicks. Its title is:

> **AI Frontier Model Builders Cheatsheet (Updated July 2026)**

Its top-earning queries are:

| Query | Impr | Clicks | Pos |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| frontier ai labs **list** | 1,264 | 25 | 4.5 |
| frontier ai models | 1,149 | 3 | 10.0 |
| frontier ai **companies** | 890 | 17 | 6.0 |
| **list** of frontier ai models | 447 | 21 | 3.6 |
| **list** of frontier ai **labs** | 405 | 4 | 3.5 |
| frontier model **companies** | 331 | 15 | 2.4 |

The words users type — **companies**, **labs**, **list** — appear nowhere in the title. "Model
Builders" is a phrase no one searches. The page already ranks 2.4–6.0 for these; it is losing the
click at the moment of choosing, not at the moment of ranking.

The same defect repeats:

| Page | Title says | Users search |
|---|---|---|
| `humanoid-robots.html` | "Humanoid Robot **Builders** Cheatsheet" | humanoid robots \| humanoid robot companies |
| `google-ai-studio-guide.html` | "**Enhanced** Google AI Studio — Settings Cheatsheet" | google ai studio **settings** / temperature / top p |
| `judo.html` | "Judo Cheatsheet: Ultimate Guide to…" | judo **guide** |
| `orbital-rockets-comparison.html` | "Orbital Rockets Comparison: Heavy-Lift…" | rocket **size** comparison \| new glenn vs falcon heavy |
| `operator-loadouts.html` | "Professional Operator Loadouts & EDC Guide" | **special forces** loadout |

**This is the highest-leverage work on the site**, and it is pure text editing.

### The counter-example that proves it

`baofeng-uv5r-quick-ref.html` earns **1.64% CTR** — 8× the flagship — on 11,496 impressions at
position 8.3. Its title (60 chars) literally contains the query: *"baofeng uv-5r programming cheat
sheet"* → the page ranks and gets clicked. `veterinary-diagnostics.html` earns **6.5% CTR**.
`ashihara-karate.html` earns 0.86%.

The pattern: **specific, low-competition, high-intent queries convert normally; broad head terms in
feature-heavy SERPs do not.** Title work pays off on the former.

## 3. Intent mismatches that no title can fix

Two pages rank well for queries whose intent they cannot satisfy. Worth naming so they are not
mistaken for fixable CTR problems:

- **`bitcoin-whitepaper.html`** — 23,208 impressions, **2 clicks**, position 9.4. People searching
  "bitcoin whitepaper" want *the original PDF from bitcoin.org*, not an explainer. This page will
  never win that query. Its value is the explainer audience, which searches differently.
- **`yudkowsky-rationality-ai-cheatsheet.html`** — ranks 4.3 for "eliezer yudkowsky" (237 impr, 1
  click). That query wants a biography/Wikipedia. Mismatch, not a defect.

Do not spend effort here.

### `anduril-products.html` — 90,225 impressions now returning 404

Removed deliberately in commit `5a5db5e` ("Remove trademark exposure from Anduril products page").
Over the 180-day window it earned **295 clicks on 90,225 impressions at position 6.6** — it was the
site's #3 page. It now returns a bare `404`.

That is defensible (the removal was intentional and legally motivated), but a bare 404 leaves Google
retrying the URL for months. **Recommendation: return `410 Gone`**, which signals permanent,
intentional removal and drops it from the index faster. Do *not* redirect to a related page — a
redirect would inherit the trademark-adjacent query traffic the removal was meant to shed.

*This needs David's decision — it's a server-config change, not a repo change.*

goal-1/goal-4 and shouldn't be pruned on search demand alone). **Deferred — flagged, not scheduled.**

### Internal linking

Every page is linked from the `index.php` gallery, so nothing is a true orphan. But **contextual**
cross-linking is thin: the flagship `ai-frontier.html` has only **5 inbound contextual links**;
`bitcoin-whitepaper.html` has 1; 23 pages have zero inbound contextual links from any sibling page.
`SEO_PROMPT.txt` defines a footer cross-linking procedure that has clearly not been applied uniformly.

**Deferred to a second pass** — it's real value but it is 151 files of judgment, and the title work
should land and be measured first.

### What success looks like

Do **not** measure this by site-wide CTR — Cause A will drown the signal. Measure:

- **Primary:** clicks on the 16 rewritten pages, 28 days before vs. 28 days after, holding position
  roughly constant. A move from 0.2% → 0.6% CTR on `ai-frontier.html` alone is ~+800 clicks/180d.
- **Guard-rail:** average position on those pages must not fall. If a rewritten title drops position,
  the title lost relevance — revert that one.
- **Re-pull:** 2026-08-06 (28 days after the change lands), same 180-day window for comparability.
