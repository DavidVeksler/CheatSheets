# Spec: The Cliff Map — Where an Extra Dollar of Income Costs You Hundreds

**Target file:** `the-cliff-map.html`

**Why this topic:** The US tax-and-benefits system contains dozens of thresholds where $1 of extra income triggers hundreds or thousands in lost benefits or added charges — IRMAA brackets, ACA subsidy cliffs, Social Security taxation tiers, credit phase-outs, FAFSA formulas. Each cliff is documented by a different agency in a different vocabulary; no one normalizes them into a single map because the compilation spans the IRS, SSA, CMS, HHS, and the Department of Education. Pure cross-source normalization — and the payoff is real money: a $100 Roth conversion that crosses an IRMAA line costs a couple ~$1,000+/yr in Medicare surcharges. Advisors sell this exact knowledge; the honest free version is a page.

## Content approach

Core distinction taught in the first 150 words: **phase-outs are slopes; true cliffs are cliffs.** A phase-out raises your *effective* marginal rate over a range (EITC taper); a cliff is discontinuous — $1 over the line reprices the whole benefit (IRMAA, the classic). The page maps both but flags which is which, because the defensive move differs (slopes: mostly ignore; cliffs: manage income to the dollar). Second recurring device: **which "income" does this cliff use?** — AGI vs. MAGI (with *which* add-backs — there are several MAGIs) vs. provisional income vs. FPL percentage; half of all cliff accidents are definition confusion.

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the master cliff table.** Every cliff, one row, sorted by threshold dollar amount (single-filer column + MFJ column): threshold, what changes when crossed, annual cost of crossing by $1, slope-or-cliff flag, income definition used, lookback year. Density: 20–28 rows. The rows:
   - **IRMAA** (Medicare B+D surcharges — 5 brackets, each a TRUE CLIFF; the $1-over cost computed per bracket for B+D combined; **the two-year lookback** — 2026 premiums price 2024 MAGI — as the headline trap; the life-changing-event appeal (SSA-44) in one line).
   - **ACA premium subsidies** (the 400%-FPL cliff when in force vs. the 8.5%-cap slope regime — whichever is current law, dated; the household-size FPL table; the repayment cliff at reconciliation).
   - **Social Security taxation** (the 50%/85% provisional-income tiers — unindexed since enactment, which is why they now bite the middle class; the "tax torpedo" marginal-rate spike explained with one worked example).
   - **Capital gains 0% bracket** (the positive cliff — harvesting gains free below the line; stacking rules in one line).
   - **NIIT** ($200k/$250k MAGI, unindexed).
   - **Additional Medicare tax** (same thresholds).
   - **Child Tax Credit phase-out**, **EITC taper** (the steepest effective-rate zone in the code for low earners — the honest note that EITC + benefits tapers can exceed 50% effective marginal rates), **Saver's Credit** (TRUE CLIFF tiers), **education credits (AOTC/LLC) phase-outs**, **student-loan interest deduction**, **IRA deductibility + Roth contribution phase-outs**, **QBI thresholds** (if current law retains them — verify), **Social Security earnings test** (pre-FRA benefit clawback — the "not really a tax" nuance in one line), **FAFSA Student Aid Index** (the simplified formula's income protection allowances; slope), **Medicaid/CHIP** (structural note — state-varying, the coverage-gap states), **savings-bond education exclusion**, **estate/gift annual exclusion adjacency** (one line, different tax).
2. **The five cliffs that move real money — deep dives.** One block each for IRMAA, ACA, SS taxation/torpedo, capital-gains 0%, and EITC-zone stacking: how the math works, a worked example arriving at real dollars (Rule 3), the management moves (see §3), and the definition-and-lookback fine print. Density: 5 blocks, each with a mini number-line diagram of its thresholds.
3. **The toolkit — managing MAGI.** The legitimate levers, each one line + when it works: Roth conversions sized *to* a bracket edge (the fill-don't-spill method), QCDs after 70½ (the IRMAA-cleanest charity dollar), tax-gain vs. tax-loss harvesting by bracket position, HSA contributions (the rare above-the-line MAGI reducer), timing bonuses/RSU sales across tax years, bunching deductions (affects taxable income, NOT most MAGIs — the classic confusion, stated), 72(t)/withdrawal sequencing in early retirement, the SSA-44 appeal after retirement/death/divorce. And the anti-move list: don't refuse raises (the "raise pushed me into a higher bracket" myth gets its own box — ordinary brackets are slopes, not cliffs; the myth-correction the whole page pivots on), don't let the tail wag the dog on sub-$100 cliff costs.
4. **The stacking section — where slopes pile up.** The scary-but-real chart concept: effective marginal rate across income for two archetype households (a 67-year-old couple drawing SS + IRA; a family of four with EITC/CTC/ACA), showing the spikes where tapers overlap — the visualization that makes the page's point that statutory brackets ≠ effective rates. Two worked archetypes only; resist the calculator temptation (Rule 4 scope control — link external tools if needed, one line).
5. **Common mistakes:** confusing the many MAGIs (IRMAA's adds muni interest; ACA's adds untaxed SS — the two readers most burned), forgetting the IRMAA lookback when planning the *first* Medicare year, December Roth conversions without a bracket-edge calculation, harvesting gains that poison ACA subsidies, assuming brackets are cliffs (raises) while missing the real cliffs (IRMAA), reconciliation surprises from mid-year income changes on ACA, and treating this page's year-tagged numbers as evergreen (the freshness line is load-bearing — read it).

Research: CMS annual IRMAA notice, IRS Rev. Proc. inflation adjustments (primary for nearly every row), SSA provisional-income rules, HHS FPL tables, current ACA subsidy statute status, FAFSA SAI formula guide. Rule 1 with maximum force: every threshold above is an anchor from memory; the build populates from the current year's primary documents, and the page ships with its tax year in the header.

## Visual design

**Topographic-map aesthetic** — the metaphor made literal: income as terrain, cliffs as contour lines packed to a drop-off. No other page on the site uses cartography.

- Palette: topo-map cream/sand base with contour-line brown, elevation-tint bands (pale green lowlands → tan → rust highlands) mapping income ranges, and **cliff-warning red** reserved for true cliffs vs. a soft slope-gray for phase-outs — the slope/cliff distinction enforced by color everywhere.
- **Signature element & og:image: the terrain profile chart** — a horizontal elevation-profile SVG: income ($0 → $500k+, MFJ and single toggle) along the x-axis, effective marginal rate as the terrain height, with the true cliffs drawn as literal vertical cliff faces (hachured edges, named summit-style labels: "IRMAA I — el. $212,000"). The og:image is this profile with 3–4 labeled cliffs. Static SVG with a no-JS fallback; the toggle is the page's one interactive element.
- **Master table rows carry map-legend glyphs:** a cliff-face icon for discontinuities, a slope icon for tapers; the income-definition column uses small badge chips (AGI / MAGI-A / MAGI-B / PROV — with a legend box, since the page's second thesis is definition confusion).
- Deep-dive blocks styled as **map insets**: bordered detail panels with their own mini number-line "trail profile" of that cliff's thresholds, benchmark-marker (▲) styling for the threshold dollar figures.
- The two stacking archetypes as side-by-side small profiles ("Route A: retired couple", "Route B: family of four") in trail-guide framing.
- The tax year in the header styled as a **map edition stamp** ("2026 EDITION — SURVEYED JAN 2026") — the freshness protocol as a design feature.
- Contour-line background texture (fine, low-contrast CSS/SVG) behind section headers only — never under body text or tables; tables stay on plain cream for legibility, 375-px safe with the threshold columns pinned left.
