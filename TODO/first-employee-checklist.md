# Spec: The First-Employee Checklist — From Offer Letter to First Paycheck, Every Agency

**Target file:** `first-employee-checklist.html`

**Why this topic:** Hiring employee #1 is a compliance event spanning five agencies that don't talk to each other — IRS, USCIS, state withholding, state unemployment, workers' comp — plus payroll mechanics, and there is no official consolidated checklist because no single agency owns the sequence. The content that exists is payroll-vendor lead-gen designed to make the process feel unmanageable ("just use us") — the opposite incentive from a clear checklist. The steps themselves are individually simple; the value is the *normalized sequence with deadlines*, which is exactly the tedious cross-agency compilation work an LLM does better than any human who'd rather sell you Gusto. And the stakes asymmetry is real: one missed registration is a letter and a small penalty, but a missed workers' comp policy plus one injury is existential.

## Content approach

The page's spine is the **swimlane sequence**: six phases (Decide → Register → Before Day One → Day One → First Payroll → The Ongoing Calendar) across four lanes (Federal / State / Insurance / Internal). Every checklist item runs the same five fields: **The step** / **Agency & form** (with form numbers — searchable handles) / **Deadline** (anchored to offer date, start date, or first payroll) / **Cost** (real fee, mostly $0 — the free-vs-scam contrast, link small-business-scams) / **Proof you're done** (the confirmation number, the certificate, the filed copy — because half of compliance anxiety is not knowing whether you finished). Density: ~30 items fully populated across the six phases.

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the swimlane checklist.** The whole sequence on one card — every item, lane-coded, deadline-annotated. This is the print artifact and the page's table of contents; each item links to its full block below.
2. **Phase 0 — Decide: employee or contractor, and what it really costs.** The classification gate in one honest block: the IRS control-factor summary and the ABC-test states one-liner (behavioral control, financial control, relationship — with the plain-language version: "if you set the hours, provide the tools, and they work only for you, they're an employee"), the misclassification penalty sketch (back taxes + penalties + state exposure), and the "trial period as contractor" trap named explicitly. Then **the loaded-cost math** — the section the reader quotes: wage + employer FICA 7.65% + FUTA + SUTA + workers' comp premium + any benefits = the ×1.25–1.4 rule of thumb, worked as a real example (a $25/hr hire's true annual cost, each line itemized, figures dated). Close with the honest alternatives paragraph: payroll service vs. PEO vs. DIY — what each actually does, generic per Rule 4, and the guidance that DIY is feasible for one employee if this checklist is followed, which is the page's reason to exist.
3. **Phase 1 — Register (before or at offer).** Federal lane: EIN (free, 10 minutes, IRS-direct — the scam warning inline); state lane: state withholding account, state unemployment (SUTA) account (the two registrations everyone conflates — disambiguated with each one's purpose and typical processing time); insurance lane: **workers' comp** — the headline block: mandatory at one employee in most states, the threshold-variance note, monopolistic-state-fund states named, ghost-policy folklore dismissed, and the stakes paragraph (an uninsured workplace injury pierces the LLC — this is the page's one existential-risk callout, visually escalated); disability-insurance states handled here.
4. **Phase 2 — Before day one.** Offer letter vs. employment contract (what belongs in an offer letter, at-will language, and what doesn't — link contract-red-flags), exempt vs. non-exempt determination (the two-part test: salary threshold — dated, flagged as in-flux — AND duties test; "salaried" ≠ "exempt" as the folklore correction), pay frequency and payday rules (state-set — verify pointer), required posters (free — link the-free-version-exists and small-business-scams), payroll setup with the deposit-schedule explanation (monthly vs. semi-weekly depositor rules in plain language), and **the trust-fund warning block**: withheld income tax and employee FICA are the government's money in your custody — the Trust Fund Recovery Penalty pierces every entity and follows owners personally; "never borrow from payroll taxes" stated as the page's second existential rule.
5. **Phase 3 — Day one.** The I-9 (within 3 business days, documentation lists in plain terms, retention rule, E-Verify where state-mandated), W-4 and state equivalent, new-hire reporting (the 20-day federal default, state variance — the step everyone misses because no one's heard of it; its child-support-enforcement purpose explained in one line so it stops seeming skippable), the day-one packet list (handbook honesty: at one employee you need policies, not a 40-page handbook — the short list that matters: pay, leave, safety, harassment).
6. **Phase 4 — First payroll & the ongoing calendar.** First-payroll verification checklist (gross-to-net walked once as a worked example — where every withheld dollar goes), then the recurring calendar as a table: per-payroll (deposits), quarterly (941, SUTA reports), annually (940, W-2/W-3 by Jan 31, workers'-comp audit, poster updates, threshold re-checks). Each with its deadline and its miss-consequence honestly sized (most are small-penalty letters — the calm counterweight to compliance anxiety, in the site's base-rate voice).
7. **Common mistakes:** the under-the-table trial period, misclassification-to-avoid-the-checklist (this page took an afternoon; the penalty takes years), skipping workers' comp at one employee, borrowing withheld taxes for cash flow (the one mistake with personal liability), missing new-hire reporting, treating any salaried worker as exempt, using an offer letter with contract-grade clauses in it, and doing all of Phase 1 but never setting up the recurring calendar (registration without deposits is where the penalty letters actually come from).

Research: IRS (EIN, Pub 15/Circular E, deposit schedules, TFRP), USCIS (I-9, current form version), DOL (FLSA thresholds — verify current litigation status, poster list), SSA (W-2/W-3), HHS/OCSE (new-hire reporting), state-agency category verification across 4–5 representative states, NCCI/state funds for workers'-comp threshold table. Rule 1: every threshold, rate, and deadline above is an anchor — verify at build and date the volatile ones inline; the FLSA salary threshold gets a "as of [date], under legal challenge" treatment if that remains true.

## Visual design

**Form-packet aesthetic** — the page as the humane redesign of the government-form packet that should exist: form-number tabs, box-grid field styling, agency plates — the visual language of official paperwork, decluttered and made friendly. Distinct from the site's case-file (small-claims) and patient-chart (hospital) document aesthetics: those are records ABOUT you; this is paperwork you're filling out, and the design celebrates completing it.

- Palette: form-paper white with **civic blue** rules and boxes (the classic government-form blue), slate ink, a warm **stamp-pad green** reserved for "proof you're done" fields and completed states, and signal red used exactly twice — the two existential-risk blocks (workers' comp, trust-fund taxes) get red-bordered plates; nothing else on the page is red, so the hierarchy is unmistakable.
- **Signature element & og:image: the swimlane sequence rail** — the six phases left-to-right, four lanes (FEDERAL / STATE / INSURANCE / INTERNAL) stacked, each step as a small form-tab chip positioned on its lane with deadline flags anchored to the offer/start/payday markers. Built as one SVG with real-text labels; og:image is the full rail with the title strap — it should read as "oh, it's just this" at a glance, which is the page's emotional promise.
- **Checklist items as form blocks:** each step styled as a numbered form section — form-number tab in the corner (the real form number: I-9, W-4, 941), the five anatomy fields as labeled boxes, an actual checkbox (functional, localStorage-persisted like death-logistics' checklist — the returning reader finds their progress), and the proof-of-done field in stamp-pad green.
- **The sticky progress rail** on desktop: a compact vertical version of the swimlane with the reader's checked items filled — the multi-session navigation the reading conditions demand. Collapses to a top progress bar at 375 px.
- The loaded-cost math as a **pay-stub-styled table** (the gross-to-net worked example rendered as a literal stub — itemized, monospace figures), foreshadowing Phase 4's first-payroll stub; the two stubs visually rhyme (employer's cost view vs. employee's deduction view — the same dollars from both sides, which is the quiet lesson).
- The ongoing calendar as a **year-wheel or 12-row ledger strip** (pick in build based on width behavior; the ledger strip prints better and print matters here).
- Print stylesheet is first-class: the swimlane card + all form blocks with empty checkboxes as a working packet — the artifact a reader literally works through with a pen over two weeks. Dark mode standard; forms keep paper-white cards on dark canvas (documents stay documents).
