# Spec: The Security Theater Audit — Which Fears Are Real

**Target file:** `security-theater-audit.html`

**Why this topic:** Every security fear generates its own ecosystem of warnings (FBI PSAs, local-news segments) and products (RFID wallets, camera covers, Faraday bags) — but almost no one goes back and asks "how many documented real-world victims does this threat have?" Answering that requires cross-domain evidence synthesis (incident reports, security-research literature, fraud statistics) that no single blogger compiles — pure LLM aggregation leverage, and the micromort page's bias-correction method applied to security. Each fear is its own long-tail query, so one page ranks for dozens of "is X real" searches.

## Content approach

The method IS the content — state it up front in a short "how we grade" block: every threat is scored on three axes: **(1) technically possible?** (usually yes — that's why the demo videos exist), **(2) documented in the wild?** (the axis that actually matters — count real victims, not proofs-of-concept), **(3) cost to mitigate?** Verdict vocabulary: **REAL** (act on it) / **EDGE CASE** (real but rare — specific populations only) / **THEATER** (technically-possible, victim-count ≈ 0) / **MISDIRECTED** (the fear is wrong but points at a real adjacent threat — the most interesting category). The page's thesis: security attention is zero-sum — every hour spent on theater is an hour not spent on the boring REAL list.

Sections:

1. **Quick reference: the verdict board.** Every audited fear in one sortable table (one row per audit — 16–20 rows, matching the section-2 roster): threat, verdict, documented-victim scale (≈0 / rare / thousands / millions), the one-line reality, mitigation if any + cost. Sorted with THEATER on top for the hook, REAL list as the payoff.
2. **The audits.** One entry per fear (the sixteen below are the floor; 16–20 audits), identical anatomy: *The fear (as circulated) / Possible? / In the wild? (the evidence, with sources and dates) / Verdict / What to do (often: nothing).* Roster:
   - **Juice jacking** (public USB chargers): FBI/FCC warnings vs. approximately zero documented wild cases; OS USB-trust prompts since ~2019. THEATER; a $10 data-blocker is fine if it makes you happy — say exactly that.
   - **RFID/NFC card skimming** ("someone brushes past with a reader"): contactless EMV is tokenized/cryptogram-per-transaction; the walk-by attack yields nearly nothing usable; documented losses ≈ 0 vs. the RFID-wallet industry's size. THEATER → MISDIRECTED: the real card threat is e-skimming (Magecart) and physical ATM/gas-pump skimmers — audit those as REAL with the wiggle-the-reader folklore.
   - **"My phone is listening to my conversations for ads":** the research (multiple studies found no evidence of mic hot-listening; the ad you saw came from location + purchase graphs + lookalike audiences). MISDIRECTED: the truth — cross-app tracking and data brokers — is creepier and actionable (cross-link privacy-data-broker-opt-out.html; permission audit steps).
   - **Hotel key cards encode your credit card** — pure urban legend (they hold room/dates ID). THEATER.
   - **Hotel room safes:** staff/admin bypass codes and default 000000 handhelds are real and demonstrated; treat the safe as protection against opportunists, not staff. EDGE CASE→REAL-ish with the honest framing: still usually your best in-room option; the folklore add-ons (safe inside locked luggage, top-of-safe seal habit).
   - **Public Wi-Fi:** the 2010s fear vs. the HTTPS-everywhere present — passive sniffing is largely dead; remaining risks (captive-portal phishing, evil-twin + fake login pages, non-HTTPS apps). EDGE CASE; the VPN-industry marketing called out by name as the fear's sponsor; what a VPN actually does/doesn't buy you.
   - **Webcam spying:** documented real cases (RATs, school-district scandal, sextortion-adjacent) but mic > camera in practice, and the cover costs $2. REAL-but-cheap-to-kill — the rational buy even at low probability; note laptop mic has no cover (the actual gap).
   - **USB drops in parking lots:** real as a *corporate* pen-test vector (cite the plug-in-rate studies ~45–50%); consumer victim count negligible. EDGE CASE (labeled: you personally, THEATER; your employer, REAL).
   - **Card skimmers at ATM/pump** — REAL, thousands of victims, with the spotting checklist (wiggle, tape seals, use inside/bank ATMs, tap>dip>swipe hierarchy).
   - **SIM swap** — REAL for anyone with crypto/valuable accounts; summary verdict here + pointer to the planned deep-dive page.
   - **Phishing/smishing ("your package/toll/bank")** — REAL at massive scale; the one-behavior fix (never act on inbound contact; go to the app/site directly).
   - **QR-code sticker scams (parking meters):** documented but modest; rising. EDGE CASE with the check-the-URL habit.
   - **AirTag stalking:** documented real cases; platform countermeasures explained. REAL (small population, serious stakes).
   - **"Thieves mark houses with symbols/zip-tie on mirror = trafficking":** viral folklore with essentially zero substantiation — THEATER, and a kind word about why these rumors spread (cross-link the micromort page's salience-bias section).
   - **Bluetooth/AirDrop attacks, car key relay** (verdict + pointer to future car page), **"shoulder surfing your phone PIN then stealing the phone"** — REAL and underrated (the WSJ-documented iPhone theft pattern; enable the anti-theft protections by name, iOS Stolen Device Protection / Android Theft Detection).
3. **The boring REAL list — where the attention should go.** The inversion payoff, half a page (6–8 threats): the ones with millions of victims that generate no PSAs because they're not novel: password reuse + credential stuffing, phishing, unpatched/EOL routers, no 2FA on email (the master key — cross-link contract of recovery paths), data-broker exposure, and unlocked doors (cross-link how-burglars-break-in.html: ~25–30% of burglaries, no force). Table: boring threat vs. the theatrical fear that steals its attention budget.
4. **The calibration heuristics.** Teach the method as portable skills: "demo ≠ deployment" (a DEF CON video is evidence of possibility, not prevalence), "who profits from this fear?" (VPN/RFID-wallet/monitoring subscriptions as fear-funded categories), "does the attack scale?" (attacks that need physical proximity to one victim don't become epidemics; attacks that run from a laptop in another country do — the single best prior), and "cheap mitigation beats probability arguments" (webcam cover logic).
5. **Common mistakes:** buying products for THEATER threats while running a reused password on email, treating all warnings as equal because an agency logo is attached (agencies warn on possibility, not prevalence), dismissing EDGE CASEs that match your profile (crypto holder ignoring SIM swap), letting debunked fears rebound as cynicism ("it's all fake") — the REAL list is real.

Research: verdicts must cite countable evidence — FTC/IC3 fraud reports for victim scales, the academic phone-listening studies, EMV tokenization docs, honest security-research write-ups. Every verdict carries a "last audited: <date>" because threat prevalence drifts (this page is a natural weekly-freshness candidate — a THEATER verdict that goes REAL must flip fast).

## Visual design

**Literal theater aesthetic** — stage, curtain, and spotlight as the design language; the verdict moment is a spotlight hitting the truth.

- Palette: deep stage-black/charcoal with theater-curtain crimson and a warm spotlight gold; art-deco playbill typography for headers (condensed caps, thin rules) — unlike anything else on the site.
- **Verdict badges as marquee signs:** REAL (lit bulbs, gold), EDGE CASE (half-lit), THEATER (dark marquee with a "🎭 THEATER" tag), MISDIRECTED (spotlight pointing off-frame — the visual pun). Consistent chip set used in the board and every entry.
- **Each audit entry as a playbill card:** the fear stated in dramatic playbill type ("TONIGHT: THE JUICE JACKER"), then the evidence block in plain clinical sans on white — the deliberate register drop from drama to data IS the page's argument, typographically.
- The verdict board (section 1) styled as a **now-showing grid** with sortable columns; victim-scale shown as a 0–4 filled-seat glyph scale (empty theater = no victims). **og:image / shareable artifact:** a 1200×630 marquee-style crop of the verdict board — a dark-marquee THEATER row beside a lit REAL row, verdict chips prominent; the full board is the "screenshot this" block.
- Section 3's boring-REAL list breaks the theme on purpose: flat, undecorated, utilitarian styling — "no costumes here" — the visual argument that real threats are boring.
- FAQ JSON-LD mandatory (see SEO note); each entry's H2 is the question verbatim.
