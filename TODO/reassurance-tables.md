# Spec: The Reassurance Tables — What Scary Symptoms Usually Are

**Target file:** `reassurance-tables.html`

**Why this topic:** Symptom search is structurally terrifying: results rank by severity-interest, not probability, so the 2 AM googler meets ALS before benign fasciculation and lymphoma before a reactive node — base-rate neglect weaponized by an attention economy. The actual prognosis base rates (~90% of back pain resolves; the overwhelming majority of palpitations, floaters, twitches, and kids' swollen nodes are benign) sit in epidemiology papers no anxious person opens, and no clinician writes the compilation because "it's probably nothing" is a liability sentence without the numbers and red-flag pairings attached. This page attaches them: every reassurance arrives *with* its red-flag list and a routing to er-triage. It's the calm twin of that page — er-triage says when to worry; this one quantifies how often you didn't need to.

Each entry's H2 is the fear-query verbatim; first sentence = the direct base-rate answer (snippet bait); FAQPage JSON-LD per entry — this joins theater-audit and free-version as the site's structured-data heavyweights.

- **Reader outcome:** For any cataloged symptom, the reader can state what it usually is with its base rate, check the red-flag list that would route them to er-triage instead, and choose among the three dispositions (watch for N days / book a routine appointment / seek care now) instead of doom-scrolling.
- **Success metric:** Breadth of fear-query organic entries + featured-snippet captures; the human tell is return-direct at odd hours (this page's real job is being the bookmark that replaces the 2 AM search spiral). Dark-mode usage will dominate — check analytics assumptions accordingly.

## Content approach

The framing contract, stated once, prominently, then never repeated per Rule 4: **this page is decision support for symptoms you've already noticed, not a diagnosis; every entry's red flags override every entry's reassurance; when in doubt, er-triage's rules win.** Tone rule for the builder: the register is a good clinician's "here's what this usually is" — warm, specific, numerate; never dismissive ("it's just anxiety"), never falsely certain. The reassurance is the *number*, not an adjective.

The entry anatomy — identical for all, the page's engine: **The 2 AM fear** (named honestly: "this twitch is ALS") / **What it usually is** (the leading benign cause, explained in two lines) / **The base rate** (the population number with source+year — e.g., benign-vs-serious fraction, resolution timeline) / **The red flags** (the specific features that change the answer — each linking to the er-triage card) / **The disposition** (watch N days–weeks / routine appointment, with what the clinician will actually do / care now). Density: **22–28 entries.**

Sections:

1. **Why symptom search terrifies — the 150-word opener.** The mechanism in three beats: search ranks by engagement, not prevalence; medical content mills enumerate worst-cases for coverage; your 2 AM brain does base-rate neglect. Then the page's promise: the same symptoms, prevalence-first. (Short. The entries are the page.)
2. **The adult tables.** Grouped by body system, each entry in the standard anatomy:
   - **Heart & chest:** palpitations/skipped beats (PVCs — near-universal on monitoring; the caffeine/sleep/stress triad; red flags: syncope, exertional onset, family sudden-death history), chest wall pain in the young (musculoskeletal/costochondritis dominance vs. the er-triage feature set), "white-coat" single high BP reading (a reading is not a diagnosis; home-monitoring protocol).
   - **Head & neurology:** the eyelid twitch and body-wide fasciculations (benign fasciculation syndrome vs. the ALS fear — the base-rate gulf; red flags: weakness/atrophy, not twitching), tension headaches vs. the thunderclap list (link er-triage), transient tingling/numbness (position/anxiety vs. the persistent-or-one-sided flags), memory lapses in midlife (retrieval slowing vs. the dementia red flags — losing keys vs. not knowing what keys do; the honest worried-well note that subjective concern warrants a routine visit, not fear), brain-fog-class fatigue (the workup reality: the boring big four — sleep, iron, thyroid, depression).
   - **Eyes & ENT:** floaters (syneresis — near-universal with age; the flashes/curtain/shower-of-new-floaters same-day list), tinnitus (highly prevalent; the unilateral+hearing-loss flag), swollen lymph nodes (reactive dominance; the size/duration/fixed/supraclavicular flags with the honest "supraclavicular is always evaluated" line), mouth ulcers vs. the 2-week non-healing rule.
   - **Musculoskeletal:** acute low back pain (the ~90%-resolution figure with its honest timeline nuance — most improve within ~6 weeks, recurrence is common and still benign; red flags: saddle anesthesia/bladder — link er-triage's cauda equina card; why early imaging usually adds anxiety, not answers — the incidental-findings segue), joint aches without injury, "growing pains" adjacency for adults (nocturnal leg cramps).
   - **Skin & surface:** new bruising (vs. the pattern that warrants counts — unexplained + petechiae + gums), moles (ABCDE + the ugly-duckling rule as the actual screen; the base rate of any given mole), hair shedding after stress/illness/postpartum (telogen effluvium — the 3-month-lag mechanism that makes it feel inexplicable), lumps under skin (lipomas/cysts dominance; the hard-fixed-growing flags), breast lumps in younger women (fibroadenoma/cyst dominance by age band with the "every new lump gets evaluated anyway" routing — reassurance and evaluation are not opposites, the page's model sentence).
   - **Gut & systemic:** blood on the paper (hemorrhoids/fissure dominance; the honest age-and-pattern lines where colonoscopy is simply the answer — no reassurance without the referral rule), night sweats (the mundane causes list vs. the drenching+weight-loss+fever triad), unintended weight change thresholds, "I found a lump/spot on a scan done for something else" — **the incidentaloma entry** (extremely common on modern imaging; the overwhelmingly benign base rates by organ; link NNT's overdiagnosis section; the disposition: follow the follow-up protocol, don't google the worst variant).
3. **The kids' table.** The parental subset, same anatomy, tone doubly calm: swollen neck nodes (shotty reactive nodes as a near-constant of childhood), fevers by number (the "height of fever ≠ severity" folklore correction, with er-triage's age cutoffs restated as the flags), innocent heart murmurs (present in ~half of kids at some point; what "innocent" means), growing pains (the classic bilateral-evening-resolves pattern vs. the unilateral/morning/limp flags), headaches in kids, milestone ranges ("is my kid behind" — the width of normal, with the real referral triggers and the free early-intervention evaluation note; link parents-threat-model), nosebleeds, constipation-driven belly pain (the great pediatric mimic).
4. **How to wait well — the watchful-waiting protocol.** The skill the entries keep prescribing, taught once: define the review date ("if still present on the 14th, I book"), one measurement beats vigilance (photograph the mole with a coin; log the palpitation episodes; the home-BP protocol), what makes a good symptom note for the eventual appointment (onset/pattern/modifiers — the er-triage handoff), the 3 AM rule (nothing on this page's watch-list needs a decision before morning — decisions made at 3 AM are made by the wrong brain), and the honest anxiety paragraph: health anxiety is itself common, treatable, and no character flaw — if the checking is the symptom, that's a real appointment too (one warm line, a pointer to care, no lecture).
5. **Common mistakes:** re-googling the same symptom nightly (the search spiral is exposure without habituation), reading base rates as guarantees (the red flags exist because someone is the denominator — the page's honesty clause), letting reassurance cancel the routine appointment the disposition called for (watchful waiting has an endpoint by definition), demanding imaging for reassurance (incidentaloma roulette — link NNT), diagnosing across entries ("twitch + fatigue + headache = surely..." — symptom-combination panic is the mill-site business model), and using this page for a symptom it doesn't list (the routing line: er-triage for acuity, your clinician for the rest).

Research: prognosis epidemiology per entry (primary literature/UpToDate-class summaries, cited with year), USPSTF/specialty-society guidance for every disposition line (AAD moles, AAO floaters, ACG bleeding, AAP fever/murmurs), the back-pain natural-history literature. Rule 1 at full force and then some: **every base rate on this page must be verified against a citable source or the entry ships without a number** — an invented reassurance statistic is this page's catastrophic failure mode, worse than no page. Single medical disclaimer per Rule 4, then the numbers do the talking.

## Visual design

**Night-to-dawn aesthetic** — designed dark-first for the 3 AM reader: a deep, calm night palette that literally warms toward dawn tones as the page (and each entry) resolves. The site's other dark pages are technical (dashboard, departure board, schematic); this one is *soft* dark — low contrast-stress, warm, unhurried.

- Palette: deep indigo-charcoal night base (not pure black — no OLED-harsh edges), warm off-white text at comfortable (not maximum) contrast, and a **dawn-gold** accent for base rates and dispositions; red-flag lists use a muted ember-red confined inside their bordered blocks — the page's only red, always boxed, never ambient. Light mode exists as the inversion (paper-warm) but dark is the design target. Generous line-height, 19px+ base, slow vertical rhythm — the typography itself should lower a heart rate.
- **Signature element & og:image: the base-rate moon** — each entry carries a large circular fill glyph (a "moon phase" donut): the benign fraction as the lit portion, the number beside it in dawn-gold numerals ("~99% benign — reactive node"). The og:image is a 3×3 grid of moons with their labels — the page's thesis (mostly lit circles) in one image.
- **Entry anatomy as a fixed card ritual:** fear stated first in quiet italic ("*it's 2 AM and you've decided it's your heart*"), then the moon + base rate, the what-it-usually-is lines, the red-flag box (bordered, ember, each flag a link to its er-triage card), the disposition as a dawn-gold footer strip (WATCH 2 WEEKS / ROUTINE VISIT / CARE NOW). Identical rhythm across all ~25 entries — predictability is calming, and the ritual is the design.
- A thin **dawn-gradient progress bar** along the page edge: night at top, warming subtly toward a dawn band at the page's end (the how-to-wait-well section sits in the lightest zone) — the page's structure as a night that ends.
- The kids' table gets a slightly lifted background tone (the nursery-nightlight variant) — differentiated, not cutesy.
- Symptom-picker index at top: plain-language chips ("my heart is skipping", "new floaters", "lump") jumping to entries — matched to how fears are phrased at 3 AM, not to body-system taxonomy (the taxonomy exists as a secondary index below).
- Interactions minimal by design philosophy: anchor jumps and the picker only; nothing animates except a slow (~4s) fade-in on the moons at first view — and even that respects prefers-reduced-motion. No pulse effects, no attention-grabbing, ever.
- 375-px single column native; moons scale down but the gold numeral never drops below prominent; red-flag boxes never truncate.
