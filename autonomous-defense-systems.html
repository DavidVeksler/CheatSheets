Quick Reference

The six facts readers search for most, answered in one line each. Details and sources are in the sections below.

CCA designations

YFQ-42A (General Atomics, "Dark Merlin") and YFQ-44A (Anduril, "Fury") are USAF Collaborative Combat Aircraft Increment 1. YFQ-48A ("Talon Blue") is real too — it belongs to Northrop Grumman, targets Increment 2, and is not yet flying.

DRFM in one line

Digital RF Memory: a jammer digitally samples an incoming radar pulse, then retransmits a delayed or altered copy to paint false targets the source radar can't easily distinguish from real ones.

Shahed‑136 engine

Runs on a MD‑550 piston engine (~50 hp), not a rocket. "Specific impulse" is a rocket-motor metric — it doesn't apply to the cruise engine, which is why no legitimate Isp figure for it exists.

EO/IR gimbal

A gyro-stabilized turret that fuses a daylight camera, thermal/IR imager, and often a laser rangefinder or designator on one gimbal mount — used for tracking, surveillance, and targeting.

"Kongsberg anti healing"

Almost certainly a mis-transcription of anti-heeling system — a Kongsberg Maritime ballast-stabilization product for ships, unrelated to air defense. Kongsberg's actual air-defense flagship is NASAMS.

Iron Dome cost

Rafael has never published an official per-interceptor price. Press estimates range roughly $40,000–$150,000+ depending on batch and surge conditions — treat any single number as an estimate.

Electronic warfare

Electronic Warfare & Directed Energy

How FPGA/SDR-based radios turn into missile jammers, what DRFM actually does, and the directed-energy systems now defeating drone swarms.

FPGA + SDR + ESM + ECM: how a missile jammer actually works

ESM (Electronic Support Measures) is the passive receive chain: it detects, characterizes, and geolocates hostile RF/radar emissions without transmitting. ECM (Electronic Countermeasures) is the active transmit chain: it jams or deceives those same threats. SDR (software-defined radio) is the wideband digital RF front end that lets one hardware chassis be reconfigured in software to do ESM one moment and ECM the next. The FPGA inside that SDR is the real-time processing core — it digitizes, buffers, and generates waveforms fast enough (nanosecond-to-microsecond latency) to keep pace with an inbound missile seeker, which a general-purpose CPU cannot do.

Vendors building FPGA/SDR-based ESM/ECM for missile-jamming and self-protection: Leonardo (Miysis DIRCM against IR-homing missiles/MANPADS; BriteCloud DRFM decoy), L3Harris (ASPEN — a software-configurable signal-processing platform across its EW/CDL lines), Leonardo DRS (DAIRCM helicopter self-protection suite), Elbit Systems (Light SPEAR / Spectrolite, built on multiple parallel DRFM channels, full-band low-band–18 GHz coverage), BAE Systems (PRISM ESM family; Nulka active missile decoy on 150+ USN/RAN/RCN warships), and Northrop Grumman (AN/ALQ-249 Next Generation Jammer Mid-Band, IOC 2021 on EA-18G Growler).

Source: Leonardo, L3Harris, Leonardo DRS, Elbit, BAE, Northrop Grumman product pages; MDPI Applied Sciences 10(12):4123 (FPGA DRFM design).

DRFM (Digital RF Memory) and electronic countermeasure types

DRFM digitally samples an incoming radar/RF waveform, stores it coherently in memory, then retransmits a delayed and/or modified copy — creating false targets or masking real ones in a way the emitting radar cannot easily distinguish from a genuine return. It is the dominant modern deception technique because it can replicate coherent, convincing false targets.

The broader ECM toolkit: noise/barrage jamming (broadband RF power overwhelms a receiver's signal-to-noise ratio), deception jamming (range-gate or velocity-gate pull-off feeds false position/velocity data), chaff (cut metallic strips creating false radar returns, tuned by length to different threat frequencies), and DRFM repeaters (above).

Named DRFM systems: Leonardo BriteCloud (expendable, self-contained DRFM jammer/decoy for fast jets), Elbit Light SPEAR/Spectrolite (multi-channel DRFM ESM/ECM). Northrop Grumman's ALQ-249 uses digital-technique generation consistent with DRFM patent literature, though Northrop's own materials don't label it "DRFM" explicitly.

Source: Wikipedia (DRFM); radartutorial.eu; Association of Old Crows; leonardo.com; elbitsystems.com.

"EO/IR gimbal market" size — and why the numbers disagree

No single authoritative government or trade-association figure exists for this market — only competing commercial market-research estimates. IMARC Group puts the global EO/IR gimbal market at $957.7M in 2025, forecast to reach $1.95B by 2034. Global Growth Insights puts it at $676.48M in 2026, forecast to reach $720.18M in 2027 (~65% defense / 35% civil split, ~40% North America share). These two paid-report publishers disagree by roughly 30–40% — typical for niche defense-market forecasting, where methodology and scope vary. Treat both as directional estimates, not verified facts.

Source: imarcgroup.com/eo-ir-gimbal-market; globalgrowthinsights.com/market-reports/eo-and-ir-gimbal-market-104186.

"Iris sensor" — which one do you mean?

In a defense EO/IR context, "Iris" maps most concretely to Anduril's Iris passive IRST/targeting sensor family (announced 2024) and, secondarily, to Robin Radar's IRIS counter-drone radar (an unrelated product from a different company). Generic "iris sensor" searches without defense qualifiers mostly surface an entirely different field — biometric iris recognition (identity scanners), which has no connection to either defense product. If you landed here from a defense search, you almost certainly mean one of the two products above, not a biometric scanner.

One-way attack drones

Loitering Munitions

"Kamikaze" or "suicide" drones that loiter over a search area before diving into a target. Specs below separate manufacturer datasheets from open-source/OSINT estimates — the latter dominate for adversary systems with no public spec sheet.

Shahed-136 "specific impulse" — why no such figure exists

The Shahed-136's cruise propulsion is the Mado MD-550, a reverse-engineered copy of the German Limbach L-550E: a 550cc, four-cylinder, two-stroke, air-cooled piston engine producing roughly 50 hp (37 kW) at 7,500 rpm, driving a pusher propeller. Specific impulse (Isp) is a rocket-motor metric — thrust per unit propellant weight-flow, in seconds — and it simply does not apply to a reciprocating propeller engine, which is instead rated by horsepower and fuel consumption. For the cruise engine, no legitimate Isp figure exists or could exist.

There's a real nuance worth knowing: the drone is launched from its rail via a small solid-fuel rocket-assisted-takeoff (RATO) booster that fires for roughly 2–3 seconds before being jettisoned — that booster technically is a rocket motor with a theoretical Isp, but no manufacturer or OSINT source (Jane's, CSIS, wreckage analyses) has ever published a value for it. It's a disposable, undocumented component.

Verified specs: length 3.5 m, wingspan 2.5 m, mass ~200 kg, cruise speed ~185 km/h, range estimated 1,000–2,500 km (DIA), warhead 40–50 kg standard (a heavier ~90 kg variant reported in later Russian production), GNSS/pre-programmed waypoint guidance. First confirmed Ukraine strike: Sept 13, 2022. A jet-powered derivative, Shahed-238/Geran-3, uses a turbojet and reaches ~600 km/h — turbojets can technically have a specific impulse figure, but none has ever been published for this drone either.

Source: Wikipedia (HESA Shahed 136, Limbach L550E); CSIS Missile Defense Project; militarnyi.com (RATO launch method); Forbes, Sept 2025 (Shahed-238).

The disputed "first autonomous AI kill" claim (Kargu-2, Libya 2020)

A March 2021 UN Panel of Experts report on Libya stated that Kargu-2-class systems were used to "hunt down and remotely engage" retreating forces, which media widely reported as the first AI-autonomous battlefield killing. This is contested: the UN report itself did not confirm the drone was operating autonomously (versus remotely piloted) during any specific engagement, nor definitively confirm a human death resulted. STM's own CEO has stated Kargu-2's autonomy is used mainly for navigation and target differentiation, with an operator required to verify and authorize any strike. Treat the "first AI kill" framing as a widely-repeated but unverified claim, not a confirmed fact.

Source: NPR, Jun 1, 2021; Lieber Institute, West Point, legal/ethical analysis.

"Kongsberg anti healing system" — what this search actually means

This is almost certainly a phonetic mis-transcription of "Kongsberg anti-heeling system" — a real Kongsberg Maritime product with nothing to do with air defense or weapons. An anti-heeling system is marine stabilization equipment: ballast tanks are interconnected via automated valves and pumps, and when a heel-angle sensor detects a vessel listing to one side, the system auto-transfers ballast water to the opposite tank to right it, letting cargo operations continue without stopping for manual correction. "Heeling" and "healing" are near-homophones, which is the likely root of the mis-transcription (a marine-parts reseller listing even misspells it the same way).

If the search intent was actually about defense, Kongsberg's real flagship air-defense product is NASAMS (below) — but there's no linguistic path from "anti healing" to NASAMS, so the marine product is the better literal match.

Source: MarineInsight, "What is Anti-Heeling System on Ships"; kongsberg.com fin-stabiliser product line.

Glossary

The acronyms and terms used throughout this page, in one place.

Core acronyms: CUAS, ESM, ECM, DRFM, SDR, FPGA, EO/IR, IRST, RATO, C2/BMS, MDS
  • CUAS / C-UAS — Counter-Unmanned Aircraft Systems; the broad category of detect/track/classify/engage layers used against hostile drones.
  • ESM — Electronic Support Measures: passive detection and geolocation of hostile RF/radar emissions.
  • ECM — Electronic Countermeasures: active jamming or deception of RF/radar/comms.
  • DRFM — Digital RF Memory: digitally samples and retransmits an altered copy of a radar/RF signal to create false targets.
  • SDR — Software-Defined Radio: a radio whose modulation, filtering, and function are set in software rather than fixed hardware.
  • FPGA — Field-Programmable Gate Array: reconfigurable hardware logic used for the real-time, nanosecond-latency signal processing ESM/ECM systems need.
  • EO/IR — Electro-Optical/Infrared: a sensor combining a visible-light (EO) camera and a thermal/infrared (IR) camera, often on a stabilized gimbal.
  • IRST — Infrared Search and Track: passive detection and tracking of targets by their heat signature, without emitting radar.
  • RATO — Rocket-Assisted Takeoff: a small, disposable solid-fuel booster used to launch a drone or munition from a rail or truck, jettisoned after a few seconds.
  • C2 / BMS — Command and Control / Battle Management System: software that fuses sensor data and coordinates units/effectors under human command.
  • MDS — Mission Design Series: the USAF's official aircraft-type naming system (e.g., YFQ-42A) — "Y" prototype, "F" fighter, "Q" unmanned.
  • Specific impulse (Isp) — A rocket-engine efficiency metric (thrust per unit propellant weight-flow, in seconds). Does not apply to piston or turbojet engines in the way it's commonly asked about.
Before you cite a number

Common Mistakes & Misconceptions

The gaps between vendor marketing, press shorthand, and confirmed government data — the errors this research pass had to specifically correct.

Don't conflate vendor claims with confirmed specs
  • Directed-energy power figures (Epirus Leonidas, RTX HELWS test-hour claims) are frequently vendor-stated with no independent audit — this page flags each one explicitly.
  • CCA speed/range/endurance numbers for YFQ-42A are simply not public; anything you see cited as a hard number for it is speculation.
  • Iron Dome's per-interceptor cost has never been officially published — treat every number in circulation as a press estimate.
Program names and designations drift fast
  • YFQ-48A is real, but it belongs to Northrop Grumman's Increment 2 contender, not a third Increment 1 aircraft alongside YFQ-42A/44A.
  • MQ-28 Ghost Bat is an Australian RAAF/Boeing program, not part of the USAF CCA competition, despite frequent conflation in casual reporting.
  • "Anvil," "Lattice," and "Pulsar" are Anduril product names, not category names — the category terms are kinetic interceptor, mission-autonomy software, and RF sensing/jamming node respectively.
Physics and terminology mismatches
  • Specific impulse is a rocket-motor metric. Asking for a piston engine's Isp (Shahed-136) is a category error — the right question is horsepower and fuel consumption.
  • "First autonomous AI kill" (Kargu-2, Libya 2020) is a widely repeated but formally unconfirmed claim — the underlying UN report does not establish it.
  • A mis-transcribed search term ("anti healing") can point to a completely unrelated product line (marine ballast systems) from the same company that also makes air-defense systems — don't assume a defense context by company name alone.
