Generator CO Rule: outside only, 20+ ft from the house, exhaust away.

CPSC's Feb. 19, 2026 safety release says portable generator CO poisoning can kill in minutes and averages nearly 100 US deaths per year. Opening windows or using fans is not ventilation. Use a transfer switch or direct appliance cords; never backfeed a wall outlet.

How to Prepare for a Power Outage

Do these before shopping: charge phones and banks, freeze water bottles, set fridge/freezer to coldest safe settings, fill containers, move meds to a cooler plan, download maps, and choose one room for thermal control.

Watt math beats generator marketing. Use running watts for what stays on, then add the largest single starting surge. Measure your own appliances with a plug meter whenever possible.

Example Essential Load

Energy Star fridge/freezer132-192 W run / 1,200 W start
Gas furnace blower300-700 W run
CPAP, no humidifier30-60 W typical
Phones, router, LED lamps50-150 W
Microwave or kettle1,000-1,500 W, run briefly

Decision: a 2-4 kW inverter unit covers the realistic list for many homes if you cycle loads. A 12 kW standby system is comfort and convenience, not baseline preparedness.

The 72-Hour Evacuation Bag

Assume car-based evacuation to a hotel, relative, shelter, or staging area. Target under about 20 lb per adult. The bag is bureaucracy, health, power, clothing, and morale.

The Two-Week Home Kit

Shelter-in-place is a system: water, food, power/light, heat/cool, sanitation, meds, comms, cash, documents. Apartments can do this with under-bed water and a closet-shelf pantry audit.

Mostly-free version: cleaned containers, pantry deepening, existing blankets, documents, cash discipline. Buy-it-all version: roughly $300-$600 before generator, depending on household size and storage choices.

Common Mistakes

These fail in ordinary emergencies, not just extreme ones.

Buying the fantasy tail first
If the CO alarm has no battery, the plate carrier is not preparedness. Finish water, alarms, meds, cash, documents, insurance, light, and comms first.
Food you never cook
Store what you eat. Run a pantry weekend: no shopping, no delivery, cook only the stored food, then fix the gaps.
Generator after the storm starts
Late purchase means no fuel, no cords, no transfer switch, no maintenance, and dangerous improvisation. Test before weather.
All preps in one place
Basement flood takes the flood kit. Split documents, water, cash, car kit, and bag so one local failure does not erase everything.
Gear still boxed
Unboxed during the event means missing adapters, dead batteries, wrong fuel, and no muscle memory. Do a 48-hour shakedown.
Prescription buffer ignored
Medical continuity is boring and hard because it involves doctors and insurance. That is exactly why it outranks new gear.
Prepping alone
Neighbors notice smoke, share tools, check on kids and elders, and relay local facts. Isolation is a fragility.
Aesthetics replacing audit
Matching pouches do not matter if you cannot name your top five emergencies, quantities, shelf lives, and failure modes.
