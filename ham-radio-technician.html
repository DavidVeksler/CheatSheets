The Strategy

Don't memorize answers blindly. Learn the concepts for electrical theory, but use rote memorization for specific FCC rules and band limits . Use the HamStudy.org "Study Mode" to focus on your weak points.

4-Week Curriculum

Week 1: Rules & Operating

Focus: The "Legal" Stuff. Memorize privileges and procedures.

T1 FCC Rules
  • License Classes: Technician, General, Amateur Extra.
  • Term: Licenses are valid for 10 years .
  • Identification: You must transmit your call sign every 10 minutes and at the end of a conversation.
  • ITU Regions: The US is in ITU Region 2 .
T2 Operating Procedures
  • CQ: "Calling any station."
  • Repeaters: Listen for input/output offsets (usually +/- 600kHz for 2m).
  • Simplex: Transmitting and receiving on the same frequency.
  • Band Plans: Voluntary agreements on where to use specific modes (voice vs data).
Week 2: The Science of Radio

Focus: The "Technical" Stuff. Math and Physics.

T5 Electrical Principles
Ohm's Law: E = I × R (Volts = Amps × Ohms)
Power Law: P = I × E (Watts = Amps × Volts)
  • Current (I): Measured in Amperes (A). Flow of electrons.
  • Voltage (E): Measured in Volts (V). Electrical pressure.
  • Resistance (R): Measured in Ohms (Ω). Opposition to flow.
  • Power (P): Measured in Watts (W). Rate of energy use.
T3 Radio Wave Characteristics
  • VHF/UHF: "Line of sight" propagation. Signals travel in straight lines.
  • HF (High Frequency): Can bounce off the ionosphere (skywave) for long distance.
  • Wavelength: Higher Frequency = Shorter Wavelength.
  • Formula: 300 / Freq (MHz) = Wavelength (meters).
    Ex: 300 / 144 MHz ≈ 2 meters.
Week 3: The Gear

Focus: Equipment, Modes, and Components.

T6 Electronic Components
  • Resistor: Limits current (Zig-zag line symbol).
  • Capacitor: Stores energy in electric field (Two parallel lines symbol).
  • Inductor: Stores energy in magnetic field (Coil symbol).
  • Transistor: Switch or amplifier (Gain).
  • LED: Light Emitting Diode (Visual indicator).
T8 Modulation Modes
  • FM (Frequency Modulation): Most common for local VHF/UHF voice. Clear audio, but signal drops abruptly (capture effect).
  • SSB (Single Sideband): A form of AM used for long-distance HF voice. Narrow bandwidth.
  • CW (Continuous Wave): Morse code. Narrowest bandwidth.
  • Digital: FT8, DMR, Packet Radio.
Week 4: Antennas & Safety

Focus: Getting signals out and staying alive.

T9 Antennas & Feed Lines
  • Dipole: Total length is approx 1/2 wavelength.
  • Vertical (Ground Plane): Omnidirectional (transmits in all directions equally).
  • Beam (Yagi): Directional antenna. High "Gain".
  • Coax Cable: RG-58, RG-8. Losses increase with frequency and length.
  • SWR (Standing Wave Ratio): Measure of match between radio and antenna. 1:1 is perfect. Above 2:1 is bad.
T0 Safety (CRITICAL)
  • RF Exposure: RF energy heats body tissue. Do not touch antennas while transmitting.
  • Duty Cycle: Percentage of time you are transmitting.
  • Grounding: Connect all equipment to a common ground point to prevent shock.
  • Tower Safety: Never climb a tower without a safety belt and helper.

Common Q-Signals
  • QTH: Location
  • QSL: Acknowledge receipt
  • QSO: A conversation
  • QSY: Change frequency
  • QRM: Interference (man-made)
  • QRN: Static (natural/weather)
  • QRZ: "Who is calling me?"
