Longevity Supplements: Evidence, Hype & Risks

No pill is proven to extend human lifespan. This ledger separates demonstrated uses from early signals, mechanisms, and marketing.

Not medical or dosing advice. Amounts below are reported study amounts or established dietary references—not a recommendation. Supplements can interact with medicines, contaminate products, and delay treatment. Correct suspected deficiency with appropriate testing and clinical guidance.

The Evidence Tier List

Tier names answer “what can we honestly claim in humans?” They do not say a product is safe, necessary, or better than food, exercise, sleep, and indicated medical care.

DEMONSTRATED USE

Creatine · omega-3 for selected indications · vitamin D deficiency correction · magnesium deficiency correction · protein/fiber adequacy

EMERGING

NMN/NR · GlyNAC · berberine · taurine · glycine · urolithin A · fisetin

UNPROVEN

Resveratrol · spermidine · CoQ10 for longevity · collagen lifespan claims · most senolytic stacks

AVOID / CAUTION

High-dose beta-carotene or vitamin E · detoxes · mega-dose stacks · unverified sexual/weight-loss products

PRESCRIPTION

Rapamycin · metformin · acarbose: intriguing animal or disease evidence, not self-experiment instructions

How to grade a supplement claim

Human outcome beats biomarker.

NAD+ going up, a mouse living longer, or a marker improving does not establish longer human life.

Deficiency correction is different.

A nutrient can be essential and valuable when deficient without improving lifespan in well-nourished people who add more.

Brand quality matters.

U.S. supplements are not pre-approved for safety, effectiveness, or label accuracy. USP/NSF certification is a quality signal, not proof of benefit.

Absence has two meanings.

“No data” is not “works”; a convincing null outcome trial is stronger evidence than a mechanism story.

Stacking, purity, and the expensive mistakes

The stack problem

Ten compounds create interactions, adherence noise, cost, and no way to know what helped or harmed. Add nothing to solve a vague “aging” feeling.

Third-party testing

USP Verified and NSF certification assess specific quality practices/products. They do not validate a claimed lifespan effect. Avoid products with hidden proprietary amounts.

Test before correction

Vitamin D, B12, iron and some electrolyte questions need clinical context. A supplement can normalize a number while hiding why it was abnormal.

Common mistakes

Buying Tier 2 while skipping Tier 0.

No NAD booster outranks tobacco cessation, activity, sleep, food, and indicated treatment.

“Clinically studied” = proven.

It may mean a short, manufacturer-funded biomarker study—read the endpoint and population.

Ignoring interactions.

Especially risky with anticoagulants, diabetes medicines, kidney disease, surgery, pregnancy, and polypharmacy.

Assuming natural means pure.

The quality and adulteration problem is part of the decision, not a footnote.
