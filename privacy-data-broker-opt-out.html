Data Broker Opt-Out Guide

A comprehensive US checklist for removing your personal information from people-search websites and data aggregators.

General Instructions & Setup

CRITICAL: Do NOT use your primary email!
Use a disposable/alias email. Create one via Firefox Relay, SimpleLogin, or DuckDuckGo Email Protection before starting.
  • For each site below, navigate to the opt-out URL provided.
  • Search for listings matching [Your Full Name] in [Your City, State].
  • If multiple listings appear (e.g., current address, old addresses in other states), opt out of ALL of them.
  • When email verification is required, check the disposable inbox and click the verification link immediately — some expire within 15 minutes.
  • If a site requires a CAPTCHA that cannot be completed, flag it and move on.
  • Do not create accounts, enter payment information, or start free trials on any site.

Professional Aggregators

Sites like LexisNexis supply data to law firms, investigators, and law enforcement. They have stricter opt-out processes.

LexisNexis / Accurint

Go to optout.lexisnexis.com to request suppression under FCRA/state law. Requires identity verification (e.g., driver's license). Takes 3-5 weeks.

Thomson Reuters CLEAR

Opt-out via their privacy portal. Similar identity verification process required.

CoreLogic

Major property records aggregator. Opt-out at corelogic.com/privacy/ under your state's privacy rights.

State Privacy Laws Leverage: As of July 2026, 21 states have comprehensive privacy laws (CA, CO, CT, DE, IN, IA, KY, MD, MN, MT, NE, NH, NJ, OK, OR, RI, TN, TX, UT, VA, and WA). Invoking your state law when submitting deletion requests creates a strict legal obligation to comply within ~45 days.
California DROP (Delete Request & Opt-out Platform): Live since January 2026 at privacy.ca.gov/drop/. California residents can submit a single request to delete personal data from 500+ registered data brokers simultaneously. Brokers must process requests by August 1, 2026.
CFPB Data Broker Rule — Withdrawn: The proposed federal FCRA rule that would have regulated data brokers as consumer reporting agencies was withdrawn May 15, 2025. No equivalent federal rule is in effect. State laws remain the primary legal leverage.
Post-Completion: Cache Removal

Even after removal from broker sites, your profile may still show up on search engines until they re-crawl the page.

  • Google: Go to Search Console Outdated Content Removal. Paste the old profile URLs you deleted.
  • Bing: Go to Bing Content Removal Tool and submit the same URLs.
Maintenance Schedule

Data brokers continuously refresh from public records (property sales, voter registration, DMV data). Re-run this entire checklist:

  • 30 days after initial completion
  • Every 90 days thereafter
  • Immediately after any major public record change (buying a house, registering to vote).
